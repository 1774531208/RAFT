Shenzhen Stock Exchange Bond Trading Rules
Chapter 1 General Provisions
1.1 This rule is formulated in accordance with the Securities Law of the People's Republic of China, the Administrative Measures for Securities Exchanges, and other laws, administrative regulations, departmental rules, normative documents, and the Articles of Association of the Shenzhen Stock Exchange in order to regulate bond trading activities, protect the legitimate rights and interests of bond investors, and maintain the order of the bond market.
1.2 This rule applies to the trading and transfer of bonds, asset-backed securities, and other products with fixed-income characteristics (collectively referred to as bond trading) conducted on the Shenzhen Stock Exchange (hereinafter referred to as the Exchange). For trading of convertible corporate bonds issued by listed companies, if the Exchange has other regulations, those regulations shall prevail. Types of bond trading conducted on the Exchange include spot bond trading, bond repurchase trading, pre-issuance bond trading, and other types of trading recognized by the Exchange. Where this rule does not provide for a specific matter, the provisions of other relevant business rules of the Exchange shall apply.
1.3 Bond trading shall adhere to the principles of openness, fairness, impartiality, voluntariness, compensation, and good faith. Bond investors (including bond trading participants and other bond investors) may independently choose bond trading products and settlement methods in accordance with the relevant regulations of the Exchange and their own trading needs.
1.4 This exchange provides facilities and related services for bond trading and conducts self-regulatory management of bond trading-related business activities in accordance with these rules and other relevant business rules of this exchange. Bond investors and institutions providing professional services for bond trading, as well as their related personnel, shall comply with the provisions of laws, administrative regulations, departmental rules, normative documents, these rules and other relevant business rules of this exchange, and the stipulations of relevant agreements when conducting bond trading and related business, and shall accept the self-regulatory management of this exchange.
1.5 The registration, custody and settlement of bond transactions shall be handled by the registration and settlement institution in accordance with relevant rules.
Chapter Two: Participating Entities
Section 1 Participants in Bond Trading
2.1.1 Members of this exchange directly become bond trading participants, while non-member institutions may apply to become bond trading participants in accordance with regulations.
2.1.2 Non-member institutions applying to become bond trading participants shall meet the following conditions: (i) possess strong capital, financial strength and sustainable operating capacity, or have the corresponding functions to participate in bond trading on the Exchange; (ii) have an independent bond trading team; (iii) have a sound trading business management system and risk control system; (iv) have relevant technical systems that can support bond trading; (v) have a large bond investment and trading volume and a strong demand for bond trading; (vi) have no major violations of laws and regulations on bond trading and settlement in the past two years; and (vii) other conditions stipulated by the Exchange.
2.1.3 Non-member institutions applying to become bond trading participants shall submit application documents to the Exchange. The application documents shall include the following: (i) basic information of the institution; (ii) registered capital, net assets, or assets under management; (iii) bond trading business management system and risk control system; (iv) the setup of the bond trading department and staffing; (v) the development of bond trading-related technical systems; (vi) bond investment and trading activities in the past two years; and (vii) other matters required by the Exchange. If a non-member institution meets the conditions stipulated in Article 2.1.2, the Exchange shall accept it as a bond trading participant within 10 trading days from the date the application materials are complete.
2.1.4 A bond trading participant may apply to the Exchange to terminate its status as a bond trading participant. The Exchange may terminate a bond trading participant's status if the participant no longer meets the Exchange's requirements or if other termination circumstances stipulated by the Exchange apply.
2.1.5 Bond trading participants may provide services such as finding counterparties and facilitating transactions in accordance with relevant regulations.
2.1.6 Bond trading participants shall establish and improve internal control systems for transaction decision-making, execution, clearing and settlement, and financial accounting, improve business processes and technical means, and strengthen internal management such as bond trading business management, compliance management and risk management.
2.1.7 Bond trading participants should handle various bond trading businesses such as proprietary trading, market making, asset management, and investment advisory separately to prevent conflicts of interest between different businesses.
2.1.8 Bond trading participants shall strengthen compliance review of bond transactions and improve bond trading compliance systems. Compliance management shall cover all relevant departments, personnel, and business processes related to various bond trading businesses.
2.1.9 Bond trading participants shall strengthen the monitoring and management of risks such as credit risk, market risk and liquidity risk in bond trading.
2.1.10 The technical systems related to bond trading of bond trading participants shall comply with the requirements of the Exchange. Bond trading participants shall formulate corresponding security operation management systems to ensure the safe and stable operation of the systems. Programmatic trading, which generates or issues trading instructions automatically through computer programs, shall comply with relevant regulations, shall not affect the security of the Exchange's systems or the normal trading order, and shall be reported to the Exchange in accordance with relevant regulations.
2.1.11 Bond trading participants shall establish a bond trading business authorization management system, strengthen the management of bond trading personnel, and assume corresponding trading and settlement responsibilities.
2.1.12 Bond trading participants shall establish contact persons for bond trading business, who shall be responsible for organizing, coordinating and contacting bond trading participants for various bond trading businesses carried out on the Exchange.
2.1.13 Bond trading participants and their related personnel shall not engage in the following activities: (i) insider trading; (ii) conducting bond trading using non-public information other than inside information in violation of regulations; (iii) market manipulation; (iv) misappropriating bond trading proceeds by taking advantage of their positions; (v) fabricating or disseminating false or misleading information to disrupt the bond market; (vi) other acts that disrupt the order of the bond market or harm the public interest; and (vii) other violations stipulated by relevant laws and regulations.
2.1.14 Bond trading participants shall submit data on bond investment transactions and reports on business operations to the Exchange. If any abnormalities occur in the bond trading or technical system operations of a bond trading participant, the participant shall promptly report to the Exchange.
2.1.15 Bond trading participants shall establish an effective settlement path when participating in bond trading on this exchange, and specific settlement matters shall be handled in accordance with the relevant regulations of the registration and settlement institution.
Section 2 Bond Market Makers
2.2.1 Bond market makers (hereinafter referred to as market makers) are bond trading participants who conduct market-making business for bond transactions in accordance with the regulations of this exchange, and are divided into primary market makers and general market makers. Primary market makers conduct continuous market-making business for benchmark market-making products (hereinafter referred to as benchmark market-making business). The benchmark market-making products will be announced separately by this exchange. General market makers conduct market-making business for market-making products they have selected for a certain period (hereinafter referred to as general market-making business). Primary market makers may conduct general market-making business. Securities companies that conduct market-making business for bond transactions shall obtain the corresponding securities business license in accordance with relevant laws and regulations.
2.2.2 Bond market making may take the following forms: (i) continuously providing two-way buy and sell quotes; (ii) responding to inquiries from bond investors; (iii) other bond market making methods recognized by the Exchange.
2.2.3 Bond trading participants who meet the following conditions may become primary market makers: (i) active market trading and ranking among the top in bond trading volume in the past two years; (ii) having a professional market-making team and strong market-making capabilities; (iii) other conditions stipulated by the Exchange.
2.2.4 Bond trading participants intending to conduct benchmark market-making business shall submit a market-making plan to the Exchange. The market-making plan shall include the following: (i) basic information of the institution; (ii) bond investment and trading activities in the past two years; (iii) the setup of relevant market-making departments and staffing; (iv) the proposed market-making products and arrangements for market-making securities accounts; (v) market-making-related risk control arrangements; and (vi) other matters required by the Exchange. The Exchange will assess the completeness of the market-making plan and announce information such as the primary market maker and its market-making products to the market.
2.2.5 Bond trading participants intending to engage in general market-making business shall report to the Exchange in advance information such as the types of bonds they intend to market-make and the arrangements for their market-making securities accounts. The Exchange shall promptly announce information about general market makers and their market-making products to the market.
2.2.6 Market makers may suspend market-making operations for the corresponding bond type if any of the following circumstances (i) or (ii) occur, or if any of the following circumstances (iii) or (iv) occur: (i) the market-making product is suspended from trading; (ii) the trading price of the market-making product experiences abnormal fluctuations; (iii) market-making operations cannot be conducted normally due to force majeure or unforeseen events; or (iv) other circumstances deemed by the Exchange as making it difficult to continue market-making operations. Market makers shall promptly resume market-making operations once the above circumstances are eliminated.
2.2.7 Market makers can enjoy support in areas such as repurchase financing, bond business innovation, transaction information, and reduction or exemption of bond transaction fees in accordance with the Exchange’s regulations when conducting market-making business.
2.2.8 Bond issuers may provide market-making support to market makers through bond re-issuance and other means.
2.2.9 The exchange will conduct regular evaluations of market makers' market-making performance and implement dynamic management of market makers.
Section 3 Other Bond Investors
2.3.1 Bond investors other than bond trading participants may act as brokerage clients and entrust members of the exchange to participate in bond trading on the exchange.
2.3.2 Before accepting a brokerage client's commission, members of this exchange shall, in accordance with regulations, fully understand the client's basic information, financial status, financial assets, investment knowledge and experience, professional capabilities, and other relevant information; truthfully explain the nature and characteristics of the relevant bond trading instruments, fully disclose investment risks, and ensure that the brokerage client complies with relevant investor suitability management regulations. Brokerage clients shall provide the truthful information listed in the preceding paragraph as expressly required by the member. If a client refuses to provide or fails to provide information as required, the member shall inform the client of the consequences and refuse to accept the commission in accordance with regulations.
2.3.3 Brokerage clients engaging in bond trading shall sign a securities trading entrustment agreement with a member of the exchange. The member shall clearly define the rights and obligations of both parties in the securities trading entrustment agreement, and stipulate matters such as the provision of brokerage client information, management of account trading permissions, verification of entrusted trading instructions, handling of abnormal trading behavior, refusal of brokerage client entrustment, and termination of the entrustment relationship.
2.3.4 Institutional brokerage clients conducting bond transactions shall be governed by Articles 2.1.6 to 2.1.11 and Article 2.1.13 of these Rules. Individual brokerage clients conducting bond transactions shall comply with the provisions of Article 2.1.10, Paragraph 2, and Article 2.1.13 of these Rules.
Section 4 Professional Service Organizations
2.4.1 Institutions engaged in bond trading intermediary services shall adhere to the principles of fairness, impartiality, honesty, and integrity, and maintain client confidentiality. They shall establish effective internal risk control systems and provide services such as finding counterparties, providing quotes, and facilitating transactions based on client requests. Institutions engaged in bond trading intermediary services shall submit relevant transaction information and statistical information related to the bond transactions facilitated to the Exchange as required by the Exchange.
2.4.2 Bond valuation agencies shall use the bond trading information of the Exchange in accordance with the Exchange’s regulations, scientifically determine and disclose bond valuation principles and methods, and reasonably determine yield curves and bond valuations.
2.4.3 Bond index compilers shall use the bond trading information of the Exchange in accordance with the Exchange’s regulations, disclose the bond index compilation method based on the principle of openness and transparency, and compile and publish relevant bond indices.
2.4.4 Institutions engaged in bond trading information services shall disseminate, use, and operate bond trading information in accordance with relevant regulations and licensing agreements, and ensure that the disseminated information is timely and accurate.
2.4.5 Institutions engaged in bond trading technology services shall comply with the technical specifications of the Exchange when providing bond market-related technical services, and shall promptly maintain and upgrade their technical systems to ensure the safe and stable operation of the technical systems.
Chapter Three: General Principles of Bond Trading
Section 1 General Provisions
3.1.1 This exchange provides trading venues and facilities for bond trading. The trading facilities consist of trading hosts, trading terminals, and related communication systems (hereinafter referred to as the trading system).
3.1.2 Bond transactions conducted on this exchange may employ matching transactions, click-to-trade transactions, inquiry-based transactions, auction transactions, negotiated transactions, and other transaction methods approved by this exchange.
3.1.3 The Exchange will arrange differentiated trading methods based on the different characteristics of bonds, such as creditworthiness, issuance method, and investor suitability. The Exchange may dynamically adjust the bond trading methods and will promptly announce them to the market.
3.1.4 The trading days for bonds on this exchange are Monday to Friday. The bond market on this exchange is closed on national statutory holidays and holidays announced by the exchange.
3.1.5 Unless otherwise stipulated by the Exchange, the trading hours for bonds under different trading methods are arranged as follows: (i) For matching transactions, the opening call matching time is from 9:15 to 9:25 on each trading day, and the continuous matching time is from 9:30 to 11:30 and from 13:00 to 15:30; (ii) For click-to-trade, inquiry-based trading, and negotiated trading methods, the trading hours are from 9:00 to 11:30 and from 13:00 to 20:00 on each trading day; (iii) For auction trading, the time for sellers to submit bid initiation declarations is from 9:00 to 10:00 on each trading day, and the time for bidders to submit bid declarations is from 10:00 to 11:30. If the market is closed during trading hours, the trading hours will not be extended. The Exchange may adjust bond trading hours according to market development needs.
3.1.6 Bond investors shall open securities accounts in accordance with the relevant regulations of the registration and settlement institution to conduct bond transactions. Investors shall use their securities accounts in accordance with relevant regulations and shall not lend their securities accounts or borrow others' securities accounts to conduct bond transactions in violation of regulations.
3.1.7 The Exchange implements an investor suitability management system for bond trading. Investors participating in bond trading must comply with the Exchange's relevant investor suitability management regulations.
3.1.8 Bond spot trading implements same-day revolving transactions, meaning that bonds purchased by investors on the same day can be sold on the same day.
Section Two: Entrustment
3.2.1 Brokerage clients may authorize members to participate in bond trading through written or telephone, self-service terminals, the Internet and other self-service methods.
3.2.2 Unless otherwise stipulated by the Exchange, brokerage clients' order instructions using the matched execution trading method shall include the following: (i) securities account number; (ii) securities code; (iii) trading direction; (iv) order quantity; (v) order price; and (vi) other content required by the Exchange and its members. The content of brokerage clients' order instructions using the click-to-execute, quote-to-execute, auction-to-execute, and negotiated-execute trading methods shall be executed in accordance with the provisions of this rule and other relevant business rules of the Exchange.
3.2.3 When a brokerage client entrusts a member to submit a declaration and complete a bond transaction, the brokerage client shall promptly deliver the corresponding bonds or funds to the member.
3.2.4 Brokerage clients may cancel the unexecuted portion of their orders.
3.2.5 If an order is revoked or becomes invalid, the member shall promptly return the corresponding funds or bonds to the brokerage client upon confirmation.
3.2.6 Members shall establish and improve investor suitability management systems and bond trading monitoring systems, and verify the bond trading entrustments of their brokerage clients in accordance with the relevant rules of the Exchange for member management. For abnormal trading activities that may seriously affect the normal trading order or are suspected of being illegal or irregular, members shall refuse to accept the entrustment from the client in accordance with the securities trading agency agreement signed with the client.
Section 3 Application
3.3.1 Bond trading participants and other institutions recognized by the Exchange shall send bond trading declaration instructions to the trading host through electronic interfaces or trading terminals. Members shall promptly submit declaration instructions for various trading methods to the Exchange based on the content of their brokerage clients' entrustments, and properly keep the entrustment declaration records in accordance with relevant regulations. Members shall be responsible for the legality, authenticity, accuracy, and completeness of the bond trading declaration instructions they issue to the Exchange and shall bear corresponding legal liabilities.
3.3.2 The Exchange’s trading system shall accept the corresponding bond trading declarations during the trading hours specified in Rule 3.1.5 of these Rules.
3.3.3 Members shall promptly submit their orders to the Exchange in the order of receipt of brokerage clients' orders. Orders and cancellations are valid only after confirmation by the Exchange's trading system.
3.3.4 Unless otherwise stipulated by the Exchange, the order quantity for bond transactions shall meet the following requirements: (i) For matching transactions, the order quantity for spot bonds shall be RMB 100,000 face value or an integral multiple thereof. Any amount less than RMB 100,000 face value when selling shall be ordered in one transaction. The order quantity for general bond repurchase agreements shall be RMB 1,000 face value or an integral multiple thereof. (ii) For click-to-trade transactions, the order quantity shall be RMB 100,000 face value or an integral multiple thereof. (iii) For inquiry-based transactions and auction transactions, the order quantity shall not be less than RMB 100,000 face value and shall be an integral multiple of RMB 1,000 face value. (iv) For negotiated transactions, the order quantity for spot bonds shall not be less than RMB 1,000 face value and shall be an integral multiple of RMB 100 face value. The order quantity for general bond repurchase agreements shall be RMB 1,000 face value or an integral multiple thereof. (v) The maximum order quantity for a single bond transaction shall not exceed RMB 10 billion face value. The exchange may adjust the requirements for the number of bond transaction declarations as needed for market development.
3.3.5 The price unit for spot bond transactions is "the price per 100 yuan face value bond," and the price unit for general bond repurchase agreements is "the annual yield to maturity per 100 yuan of funds." Exceptions apply if otherwise stipulated by the Exchange.
3.3.6 For transactions using the matched transaction method, the minimum price increment for spot bond orders is RMB 0.001, and the minimum price increment for general bond repurchase agreements is RMB 0.005; for other transaction methods, the minimum price increment for bond orders is RMB 0.0001. Exceptions apply where otherwise stipulated by the Exchange. The Exchange may adjust the minimum price increment as needed based on market developments.
3.3.7 Bond spot transactions shall be submitted using the net price, unless otherwise stipulated by the Exchange to be submitted using the full price. The net price refers to the price excluding accrued interest; the full price refers to the price including accrued interest.
3.3.8 No price fluctuation limits apply to bond trading. Unless otherwise stipulated by the Exchange.
3.3.9 Settlement methods for bond transactions include multilateral net settlement and transaction-by-transaction full settlement. Bond investors choosing a settlement method and settlement cycle must comply with the regulations and requirements of the Exchange and the clearing and settlement institution. When using transaction-by-transaction full settlement as the settlement method in a bond transaction declaration, bond investors may choose a settlement cycle. The settlement date for spot bond transactions and the first settlement date for bond repurchase transactions shall not be later than the third trading day following the transaction date (inclusive). Exceptions are made by the Exchange. For bond transactions declared between 15:30 and 20:00, the settlement method must be transaction-by-transaction full settlement, and the settlement date for spot bond transactions (or the first settlement date for bond repurchase transactions) shall not be the transaction date itself.
3.3.10 When a matched transaction is adopted, bond investors shall submit their applications anonymously; when a negotiated transaction is adopted, bond investors shall submit their applications publicly; when other transaction methods are adopted, bond investors may choose to submit their applications anonymously or publicly.
3.3.11 Bond trading orders submitted on the same day are valid for the same day, unless otherwise stipulated by the Exchange. For transactions using the matching or click-to-execute method, if a bond trading order cannot be fully executed at once, the unexecuted portion remains valid for the same day.
3.3.12 Except for the time periods during which cancellation of orders is not accepted as specified in these rules, during other times when orders are accepted, unexecuted orders may be cancelled; if a partial order is executed, the unexecuted portion may be cancelled. Cancellation orders are valid only upon confirmation by the trading system.
Fourth Section Transactions
3.4.1 Bond transaction declarations shall be made in accordance with the relevant provisions of laws, administrative regulations, departmental rules, normative documents and these rules, as well as the risk control requirements and transaction needs of bond investors, and shall be completed in accordance with the principles of price competitiveness, quantity matching and time priority.
3.4.2 A transaction is established upon successful execution of a transaction application through the trading system. Transactions executed in accordance with these rules become effective upon establishment. The transaction result is based on the transaction data recorded by the trading system, and both parties to the transaction shall acknowledge the transaction result and fulfill their clearing and settlement obligations, unless otherwise stipulated by the Exchange.
3.4.3 For bond transactions conducted through competitive bidding or other methods settled via multilateral net settlement, the transaction cannot be cancelled or the result changed after confirmation by the trading system, except in the circumstances stipulated in Article 8.1 of these rules. For transactions conducted via click-to-trade, inquiry-based trading, or negotiated trading settled on a transaction-by-transaction basis, if the transaction objective cannot be achieved or the transaction cannot continue due to force majeure, anticipated default, or delayed performance, the relevant transaction may be cancelled upon mutual agreement between the parties before the commencement of settlement.
3.4.4 For the transfer of privately placed bonds, the total number of investors holding bonds issued in the same offering shall not exceed the number stipulated by laws, administrative regulations, departmental rules or the business rules of this exchange.
3.4.5 For bond transactions using the full settlement method on a transaction-by-transaction basis, bond investors can set the range of counterparties through the trading system. If a transaction is submitted by one party that is not within the range of counterparties, the trading system will not confirm it.
Chapter Four: Bond Trading Methods
Section 1 Matching Transactions
4.1.1 Matched execution refers to a trading method in which the trading system automatically matches bond trading orders according to the principles of price priority and time priority, and in accordance with the relevant provisions of these rules. The price priority principle at the time of execution is as follows: higher-priced buy orders take precedence over lower-priced buy orders, and lower-priced sell orders take precedence over higher-priced sell orders. The time priority principle at the time of execution is as follows: for trades with the same direction and price, those submitted earlier take precedence over those submitted later. The order is determined by the time the trading system accepts the orders.
4.1.2 Matching transactions employs two methods: aggregated matching and continuous matching. Aggregated matching matches all transaction orders received within a specified time period in a single, centralized manner. Continuous matching matches each received transaction order one by one. Transaction orders that are not matched during aggregated matching are automatically entered into continuous matching.
4.1.3 When using the matched order method, limit orders or other order types approved by the Exchange may be used. Limit orders are executed at prices better than or equal to the limit price.
4.1.4 Limit order elements shall include securities account number, securities code, trading direction, quantity, and price. Orders shall be transmitted in accordance with the format prescribed by the Exchange. The Exchange may adjust the content of orders as needed by the market.
4.1.5 During the opening call matching phase, the valid bid price range for bonds is 30% above and below the previous closing price. During the continuous matching and intraday temporary suspension and resumption call matching phases, the valid bid price range for treasury bonds, local government bonds, government-supported bonds, and policy bank bonds is 10% above and below the most recent matched transaction price; for other bonds, the valid bid price range is 20% above and below the most recent matched transaction price. Exceptions apply where otherwise stipulated in these rules. On the first day of a bond's listing, the issue price is used as the previous closing price. During the opening call matching phase, the valid bid price range for general bond repurchase agreements is 100% above and below the previous closing price. During the continuous matching and intraday temporary suspension and resumption call matching phases, the valid bid price for general bond repurchase agreements shall not exceed 100 basis points above the most recent matched transaction price. Exceptions apply where otherwise stipulated by the Exchange. If there is no trading on the day, the previous closing price is used as the most recent transaction price. The valid bid price range calculation result is rounded to the smallest price fluctuation unit. If the absolute value of the difference between the upper or lower limit of the valid bid price range and the most recent transaction price is lower than the minimum price fluctuation unit, the valid bid price range shall be the most recent transaction price plus or minus one minimum price fluctuation unit of the bond.
4.1.6 Declarations exceeding the valid price range are invalid.
4.1.7 If no transaction is reached during the bond matching phase, the valid bid price range will be adjusted as follows during continued trading: if the highest bid price is higher than the previous closing price displayed in the real-time market data or the most recent matched transaction price, the valid bid price range will be adjusted based on the highest bid price; if the lowest ask price is lower than the previous closing price displayed in the real-time market data or the most recent matched transaction price, the valid bid price range will be adjusted based on the lowest ask price. The Exchange may adjust the valid bid price range as needed by market developments.
4.1.8 During the set-matching phase, the principle for determining the transaction price is as follows: (i) the price that achieves the maximum trading volume; (ii) all buy orders above the price and all sell orders below the price are executed; (iii) at least one side (buyer or seller) at the same price is fully executed. If two or more prices meet the above conditions, the price with the smallest difference between the cumulative number of buy orders above the price and the cumulative number of sell orders below the price is taken as the transaction price; if the difference between the cumulative number of buy and sell orders is still equal, the price closest to the previous closing price displayed in the real-time market is taken as the transaction price during the opening set-matching phase, and the price closest to the most recent transaction price is taken as the transaction price during the intraday set-matching phase after a temporary suspension and resumption of trading. All transactions during the set-matching phase are executed at the same price.
4.1.9 When there is continuous matching, the principle for determining the transaction price is as follows: (i) if the highest buy order price is the same as the lowest sell order price, the transaction price shall be the same; (ii) if the buy order price is higher than the lowest sell order price in the centralized order book at that time, the transaction price shall be the lowest sell order price in the centralized order book at that time; (iii) if the sell order price is lower than the highest buy order price in the centralized order book at that time, the transaction price shall be the highest buy order price in the centralized order book at that time.
4.1.10 During the opening call matching phase from 9:20 to 9:25 on each trading day, the exchange's trading system does not accept cancellation requests for matched trades.
Section 2 Click to Complete
4.2.1 Click to trade refers to a trading method in which the quoting party submits a quotation, the receiving party clicks on the quotation, and the trading system confirms the trade, or the trading system automatically matches the trades according to the relevant provisions of these rules.
4.2.2 When using the click-to-trade method, the quotation can be sent to the entire market or to a subset of bond investors selected by the quoting party.
4.2.3 The elements of the quotation submitted by the quoting party shall include the securities code, trading direction, price, quantity, sending scope, securities account number, settlement method, settlement cycle, and whether it is anonymous.
4.2.4 The bidding party can set a requirement for full acceptance of the declared quantity. Full acceptance means that the relevant bid will only be confirmed as a transaction if the bidder accepts the entire declared quantity in one go; partial acceptance is not accepted.
4.2.5 Bidders may set their bids to display only a portion of the submitted quantities at a time (hereinafter referred to as the "displayed quantity"), with the total quantity of a single transaction not exceeding the displayed quantity. After the displayed quantity is fully executed, the lower of the set displayed quantity and the remaining submitted quantities will continue to be displayed until the entire submitted quantity is displayed and executed. The set displayed quantity shall comply with the relevant provisions of Article 3.3.4 of these rules regarding the quantity of bids that are executed upon clicking.
4.2.6 The receiving party shall enter the quantity of the bid to be accepted in the opposite direction of the transaction direction quoted by the quoting party. This quantity shall comply with the relevant provisions of Articles 3.3.4, 4.2.4, and 4.2.5 of these rules. Clicking "Transaction" will complete the transaction based on the quantity entered by the receiving party, the price quoted by the quoting party, the settlement method, the settlement period, and other bidding elements.
4.2.7 After submitting a bid, the bidder may modify the price, quantity, and other bid elements for the unsuccessful portion of the bid.
4.2.8 For quotes that are settled in a multilateral net settlement manner and meet the following conditions, the quoter may choose to participate in the matching of limit orders at the hourly point during the continuous matching phase of the Exchange in accordance with the relevant continuous matching regulations: (i) the quote is sent to the entire market; (ii) the quoted price and quantity comply with the relevant matching regulations; (iii) no full-amount matching requirement is set; (iv) other conditions stipulated by the Exchange.
Section 3 Inquiry and Transaction
4.3.1 Quotation-based transaction refers to a transaction method in which a bond investor, as the quoting party, sends a quotation request to a market maker or other bond trading participant and selects one or more quotation responses to confirm the transaction.
4.3.2 The requesting party may send a request for quotes to market makers or other bond trading participants. The request for quotes should include the security code, trading direction, quantity, target audience, securities account number, settlement method, settlement cycle, and whether it is anonymous.
4.3.3 The party receiving the quote shall respond to the quote request in the opposite direction of the requested transaction, based on the elements of the quote request. The elements of the quote response shall include price, quantity, securities account number, etc.
4.3.4 After the inquiring party selects and confirms the inquiry response, the transaction shall be completed according to the quantity, price, settlement method and settlement cycle confirmed by the inquiring party.
4.3.5 The inquiring party may withdraw an unsuccessful inquiry request, and the respondent may withdraw an unsuccessful inquiry response. Once an inquiry request is withdrawn, the response to that inquiry is also automatically withdrawn.
4.3.6 Bond investors may send intention declarations to the entire market or to a subset of bond investors. Intention declarations cannot be directly confirmed for execution. Bond investors who receive intention declarations can complete transactions with the sender of the intention declaration through methods such as price inquiry or negotiated execution. The elements of an intention declaration should include the security code, transaction direction, sending scope, security account number, and whether it is anonymous.
Fourth Section Bidding Results
4.4.1 Auction transaction refers to a transaction method in which the seller sells bonds to one or more bidders with the best bid within a limited time and in accordance with the established auction transaction rules.
4.4.2 Bidding transactions can be completed using methods such as single-entity winning bids or multi-entity winning bids. In the single-entity winning bid method, the highest bidder wins the bid at its offered price and for all quantities declared in that bid. Multi-entity winning bids include single-price winning bids and multiple-price winning bids. In the single-price winning bid method, all winning bids are executed at the marginal price. In the multiple-price winning bid method, all winning bids are executed at their respective offered prices.
4.4.3 The seller shall make an appointment with the Exchange in advance to participate in the bidding and specify the bidding date. The appointment details shall include the following: bidding method, security code, price range, quantity, and bidding time. Prior to the bidding date, the seller shall organize bond investors according to its own needs to ensure a smooth and orderly bidding process.
4.4.4 Before the bidding date, the seller may modify the bidding reservation details or cancel the reservation.
4.4.5 Before the bidding date, the seller shall, based on the reservation situation, release bidding information to the market through this exchange.
4.4.6 The seller shall submit a bid initiation declaration through the trading system on the bidding date. The bid initiation declaration shall include the bidding method, security code, price range, quantity, minimum total transaction volume (if any), security account number, settlement method, settlement cycle, and whether anonymity is required. In principle, the elements of the bid initiation declaration should be consistent with the elements of the corresponding bid reservation.
4.4.7 During the bidding period, bond investors may submit bidding orders as bidders. Bid order elements should include the security code, price, quantity, security account number, and whether the bid is anonymous.
4.4.8 After the deadline for submitting bids, the trading system calculates the transaction price and quantity according to the corresponding transaction principles for the bidding method, and generates transaction information. For single-entity bidding, the bidder with the best bid will complete the transaction for the entire quantity bid. If there are multiple bids for the best bid, they will be executed according to the time priority principle. For multi-entity bidding, the trading system sorts the effective bids from best to worst price and summarizes the cumulative quantity of bids. If the cumulative quantity of bids does not reach the minimum total transaction volume, all bids will not be executed. If the cumulative quantity of bids is not less than the minimum total transaction volume but does not reach the total bidding volume, the lowest price of the bids will be the marginal price, and all bids will be executed according to their bid quantities. If the cumulative quantity of bids reaches the total bidding volume, the price at which the total bidding volume is reached will be the marginal price. All bids with prices better than the marginal price will be executed, and the transaction quantity will be the corresponding bid quantity. Bids at prices equal to the marginal price are partially or fully executed. The initial allocation of the marginal winning bids is performed by weighting each bid and rounding down. After the initial allocation, any remaining quantities are allocated according to the time priority principle.
4.4.9 After the bidding process is completed, the exchange will promptly release information on the bidding results. For bids won by a single entity, information such as the quantity won and the price won will be released. For bids won by multiple entities, information such as the quantity won and the marginal price will be released.
4.4.10 Before a bidder submits a valid bid, the seller may withdraw its bid upon application and with the approval of the Exchange. In a single-entity bidding process, bids are irrevocable. In a multi-entity bidding process, bids may be withdrawn before the bid submission deadline.
4.4.11 If trading is temporarily suspended during the bidding period specified in Rule 3.1.5 of these Rules, all bond bidding will cease for the day. If a bond is suspended from trading during the bidding period specified in Rule 3.1.5 of these Rules, bidding for that bond will cease for the day. Exceptions apply if otherwise stipulated by the Exchange.
Section 5 Negotiated Transaction
4.5.1 Negotiated transaction refers to a transaction method in which bond investors reach a bond transaction intention through negotiation or other means, submit it to the trading system, and the transaction is confirmed by the trading system.
4.5.2 Bond investors may, in accordance with the relevant provisions of laws, administrative regulations, departmental rules, normative documents and these rules and internal risk control requirements, entrust intermediary service institutions or find counterparties on their own, and reach an agreement on the elements of bond transactions in accordance with the principles of bond transaction settlement.
4.5.3 The declaration elements for negotiated transactions shall include the securities code, transaction direction, price, quantity, securities account number, information of the party and the counterparty, settlement method, settlement cycle, etc.
4.5.4 Negotiated Transactions are concluded based on the price, quantity, settlement method, settlement period, and other elements agreed upon by both parties. One party sends the agreed transaction elements to the counterparty through the transaction system. After confirmation by the counterparty, the transaction system confirms the transaction.
4.5.5 If a market maker or other institution recognized by the exchange reaches two trading intentions with different counterparties for the same trading instrument, with the same quantity but opposite trading directions, the two trades may be combined and submitted to the trading system. The elements of the submission shall comply with the provisions of Article 4.5.3 of these Rules. After the combined submission is confirmed by the counterparties of the two trades respectively, the trading system shall confirm the transaction.
Chapter Five Other Transactions and Services
Section 1 Listing, Delisting, Suspension and Resumption of Trading
5.1.1 This exchange will list and trade bonds that meet the listing and trading requirements.
5.1.2 In accordance with the relevant business rules of this Exchange, if a bond is delisted or transferred, this Exchange shall delist it.
5.1.3 If bond trading experiences abnormally large fluctuations in trading prices, which have a significant adverse impact on the reasonable pricing of the relevant bonds, or other circumstances that seriously affect the trading order of the bond market, the Exchange may suspend trading of the relevant bonds as appropriate. Trading will resume after the relevant circumstances are resolved.
5.1.4 If a bond is temporarily suspended from trading before 9:25 AM, upon resumption of trading on the same day, accepted orders will be matched using an opening call auction, and trading will continue for the rest of the day. If a bond is temporarily suspended from trading at 9:30 AM or later, upon resumption of trading on the same day, accepted orders will be matched using an intraday call auction, and trading will continue for the rest of the day. During the temporary suspension period for matched orders, orders can be submitted and cancelled. During the temporary suspension periods for click-to-trade, inquiry-based trade, and negotiated trade methods, the exchange will not accept corresponding trade orders, but will accept cancellation orders that meet the requirements.
5.1.5 If a bond is suspended from trading for the entire day, the trading system will not accept related transaction submissions during the suspension period.
5.1.6 During the period when a bond is suspended from trading, the market data released by the exchange includes information about the bond; after the bond is delisted, the market data will no longer include information about the bond.
5.1.7 Other provisions regarding the listing, delisting, suspension, and resumption of trading of bonds shall be implemented in accordance with the Exchange's bond listing rules, listing rules, and other relevant regulations.
Section Two: Other Services
5.2.1 For bonds with conversion or exchange clauses, bondholders may submit conversion (exchange) applications through the trading system or other methods recognized by the exchange during the conversion (exchange) period to convert their bonds into corresponding stocks or shares.
5.2.2 For bonds with a put option for investors, when the put option conditions are met, bondholders may submit a put option application during the put option application period through the trading system or other methods approved by the Exchange, putting back all or part of their bonds to the issuer. Put option applications can be withdrawn on the same day. If a bondholder does not submit a put option application or withdraws their put option application during the put option application period, they are deemed to continue holding the bonds. If the issuer intends to conduct bond resale after the put option is exercised, it may do so through the trading system or other methods approved by the Exchange, according to relevant arrangements.
5.2.3 Bond installment repayment shall be handled by reducing the actual face value. The Exchange may reduce holdings or use other methods as needed based on market conditions. When bond installment repayment is handled by reducing the actual face value, the following provisions shall apply: (i) The number of bonds held remains unchanged, and the actual face value of the bonds is reduced accordingly based on the proportion of repayments. The calculation formula is: Actual face value = RMB 100 × Outstanding proportion; (ii) The Exchange shall perform ex-rights processing on the trading day following the record date. The calculation formula for the ex-rights reference price is: Ex-rights reference price = Previous closing price - RMB 100 × Repayment proportion; (iii) If the bond issuer deems it necessary to adjust the above formula, it may submit an adjustment application to the Exchange and explain the reasons. If the Exchange agrees, the bond issuer shall publish the ex-rights reference price calculation formula applicable to this ex-rights transaction to the market; (iv) The previous closing price of the bond displayed in the real-time market on the ex-rights date shall be the ex-rights reference price, unless otherwise stipulated by the Exchange. When bond installment repayment is handled by reducing holdings, the actual face value of the bonds remains unchanged, and the number of bonds held is reduced accordingly based on the proportion of repayments. If the reduction amount calculated accordingly is not an integer multiple of the face value of 100 yuan, the repayment will be made according to the principle of rounding down. The remaining outstanding amount will continue to exist and enjoy corresponding rights as stipulated in the offering memorandum. Specific details regarding other methods of installment repayment of bonds will be separately stipulated by the exchange.
5.2.4 When a bond traded at full price pays interest, the Exchange will process the ex-interest adjustment on the trading day following the record date, unless otherwise stipulated by the Exchange. The previous closing price of the bond displayed in the real-time market data on the ex-interest date is the ex-interest reference price, where the ex-interest reference price = previous closing price - interest paid this time.
5.2.5 Bond investors may submit a transfer application through the exchange's trading system to transfer their listed and traded cross-market bonds between different registration and settlement institutions.
5.2.6 Member brokerage clients may purchase bonds through the same securities account at different securities branches of one or more members, and the purchased bonds may be transferred and held in custody between different securities branches of one or more members. Specific regulations shall be formulated by the clearing and settlement institution.
5.2.7 The Exchange may provide other related services through the trading system based on market developments and investor needs.
Chapter Six Transaction Information
6.1 The Exchange or its authorized institutions shall publish bond trading information, bond transaction information, basic bond market information, bond indices, bond yield curves, and other trading information to the entire market on each trading day, unless otherwise stipulated by the Exchange.
6.2 The Exchange will release bond transaction information to the market in real time. For transactions using the matching method, the Exchange will release information such as reference price, matched volume, and unmatched volume during the aggregate matching phase, and information such as the best five bid and ask prices and quantities during the continuous matching phase. During the temporary suspension of bond trading, the aggregate matching reference price, matched volume, and unmatched volume will not be disclosed. Unless otherwise stipulated by the Exchange, the Exchange will release the following information sent by bond investors to the entire market on a transaction-by-transaction basis: (i) information such as the time, price, and quantity of large-volume buy and sell orders for matched bonds; (ii) information such as the time, quantity, and price of bids submitted by the bidders who clicked on the transaction; (iii) information such as the time, quantity, and price (if any) of intention bids; (iv) information such as the time, bidding method, quantity, and price range of bids submitted for successful bids, and the time and price of bids submitted for single-entity winning bids.
6.3 Unless otherwise stipulated by the Exchange, the Exchange shall publish the following bond trading information in real time: (i) real-time bond trading data; (ii) transaction data for click-to-trade, inquiry-based, auction-based, and negotiated-trade transactions; and (iii) large-amount transaction data for bond spot matching transactions. Real-time bond trading data includes the previous closing price, opening price, most recent transaction price, most recent matching transaction price, highest price of the day, lowest price of the day, weighted average price of the day, cumulative trading volume of the day, cumulative trading volume of matching transactions, and cumulative trading amount of the day. The previous closing price displayed in the real-time bond trading data is the closing price of the security on the previous trading day, except in the following circumstances: (i) on the first day of listing/ex-trading of the bond, the previous closing price displayed in the real-time data is its issue price; (ii) on the ex-rights (interest) date of the bond, the previous closing price displayed in the real-time data is the ex-rights (interest) reference price of the bond; and (iii) other circumstances stipulated by the Exchange. Transaction data for each bond transaction includes the trading method, transaction price, and transaction time.
6.4 Bond trading participants shall use market information within the scope permitted by the Exchange.
6.5 This Exchange publishes the opening and closing prices for spot bond transactions and general bond-backed repurchase transactions. The transaction data used in calculating the opening and closing prices are the transaction data achieved through various transaction methods, unless otherwise stipulated in these rules. The opening price is the price of the first transaction of the day. The closing price is the volume-weighted average price of the transactions within one hour prior to the last transaction (inclusive) before 15:30 on the day; transaction data after 15:30 is not included in the closing price calculation. If there are no transactions on a given day, the previous closing price will be used as the closing price for that day. The Exchange may adjust the calculation methods for the opening and closing prices as needed by market developments.
6.6 The Exchange releases basic information about the bond market to the market, including basic bond information, information on bond trading participants, information on market makers and market-making products, etc.
6.7 This Exchange or its authorized institutions may compile various bond indices to reflect changes and trends in the overall price of bonds or the price of a particular type of bond, and publish them in real time. Specific plans for the establishment and compilation of bond indices will be separately stipulated by this Exchange.
6.8 This Exchange or its authorized institutions may compile various bond yield curves to reflect the overall level of bond yields and release them to the market in a timely manner. Specific plans for the setup and compilation of bond yield curves will be separately stipulated by this Exchange.
6.9 All transaction information generated in the bond market of this Exchange belongs to this Exchange. No institution or individual may use or disseminate it without permission. Institutions and individuals authorized by this Exchange to use transaction information may not provide such information to other institutions or individuals for use or disseminate it without consent. The management regulations for transaction information will be formulated separately by this Exchange.
6.10 The Exchange may adjust the timing, content, and method of releasing bond trading information based on market developments.
Chapter Seven: Supervision of Transaction Activities
7.1 The Exchange will focus on monitoring the following matters in bond trading: (i) suspected insider trading, market manipulation, trading using undisclosed information, and other illegal or irregular activities; (ii) bond trading activities where the timing, quantity, and method are restricted by laws, administrative regulations, departmental rules and normative documents, and the Exchange's business rules; (iii) abnormal trading activities that may affect bond trading prices or trading volumes; (iv) situations where bond trading prices or trading volumes are significantly abnormal; and (v) other matters that the Exchange deems necessary to monitor.
7.2 Abnormal trading behaviors that may affect bond trading prices or trading volumes include: (i) false declarations, i.e., submitting and canceling a large number of orders without the intention of completing a transaction, in order to induce, mislead, or influence the normal trading decisions of other bond investors; (ii) price manipulation, i.e. submitting large, continuous, or intensive orders, or submitting orders at prices that are significantly deviating from the latest transaction price or reasonable value of the bond, during which the bond trading price rises or falls significantly; (iii) large or frequent self-buying and selling, acting as counterparties, or reverse transactions between individual accounts, accounts under one's actual control, or related accounts, affecting bond trading prices or trading volumes; (iv) trading instructions automatically generated or issued by computer programs. (v) Programmatic trading that affects the security of the Exchange's system or the normal trading order; (vi) Quotations that deviate significantly from reasonable market prices to induce or mislead other bond investors' trading decisions; (vii) Trading prices that deviate significantly from the latest transaction price or reasonable value, suspected of transferring benefits through bond trading; (viii) During the bond price-sensitive period, abnormal reporting that affects the trading price, settlement price, or reference value of related securities or their derivatives; (ix) A large number of continuous transactions of a single bond within a period of time; (ix) Using trading in other related markets to influence bond trading, or using bond market trading to influence trading in other related markets; (x) Other abnormal trading behaviors that the China Securities Regulatory Commission or the Exchange deems necessary to monitor closely. The Exchange will conduct combined monitoring of single or multiple ordinary securities accounts, credit securities accounts, and other securities accounts (groups) suspected of being related, opened in the name of the investor or actually controlled by the same investor.
7.3 Situations where bond trading prices or trading volumes are significantly abnormal include: (i) a significant increase or decrease in bond trading prices on a single trading day or within a period of time, which is significantly deviated from the reasonable value; (ii) a significant increase in bond trading volume on a single trading day or within a period of time compared to the previous period, and the company has not made any announcements regarding major events; (iii) other abnormal trading situations that the Exchange deems necessary to monitor closely.
7.4 As needed by the market, this exchange may, in conjunction with other securities and futures exchanges and other institutions, conduct investigations into situations such as those described in Article 7.2, paragraphs (vii) and (ix).
7.5 The Exchange may conduct on-site or off-site investigations into key matters under its supervision in bond trading, and require relevant members and their securities business departments, other bond trading participants, and bond investors to provide the following documents and materials in a timely, accurate, and complete manner: (i) the investor's account opening information, power of attorney, fund account information, and transaction information of related securities accounts; (ii) the actual controller and actual operator of the relevant securities account or fund account, the source of funds, and an explanation of the related account relationships; (iii) the investor's explanation of specific trading behaviors; and (iv) other documents and materials required by the Exchange.
7.6 Members shall monitor the bond trading activities of their brokerage clients in real time. If any abnormal trading activities are detected, members shall promptly notify, remind, or warn the clients. For abnormal trading activities that may seriously affect the normal trading order or are suspected of being illegal or irregular, members shall refuse to accept the client's commission in accordance with the trading agency agreement and report it to the exchange in a timely manner.
7.7 Bond trading participants shall establish a multi-indicator, differentiated benchmark system for comparing bond spot trading prices. Such benchmarks include, but are not limited to, valuations by bond valuation agencies and other fair indicators recognized by the Exchange, unless otherwise stipulated by the Exchange. If a bond trading price deviates from the benchmark by more than the range stipulated by the Exchange, the bond trading participant shall submit to the Exchange an explanation of the reasons for and the reasonableness of the price deviation. The Exchange may, depending on the actual circumstances, require bond trading participants and related parties to submit relevant explanations regarding other special circumstances of price deviation.
7.8 The Exchange will monitor the risks of bond trading. In the event of significant abnormal fluctuations, the Exchange may take measures such as restricting trading or suspending trading, and will report to the China Securities Regulatory Commission; if the fluctuations seriously affect market stability, the Exchange may take measures such as temporarily suspending trading and will issue a public announcement.
Chapter 8 Handling Abnormal Transaction Situations
8.1 In order to maintain the normal order of bond trading and market fairness, the Exchange may decide to take measures such as technical suspension or temporary market closure if the following unforeseen events cause some or all bond transactions to be unable to proceed normally: (i) force majeure; (ii) unforeseen events; (iii) major technical failures; (iv) major human errors; (v) other abnormal circumstances as determined by the Exchange. If the unforeseen events stipulated in the preceding paragraph cause a major abnormality in the bond trading results, and settlement according to the trading results would have a significant impact on the normal order of bond trading and market fairness, the Exchange may take measures such as canceling the transaction or notifying the registration and settlement institution to suspend settlement.
8.2 If the Exchange believes that an abnormal trading situation as stipulated in Article 8.1 may occur and seriously affect the normal conduct of trading, it may decide to suspend trading technically or temporarily suspend the market. Upon the request of the China Securities Regulatory Commission, the Exchange implemented a temporary market suspension.
8.3 The Exchange shall promptly report its decisions regarding technical suspensions, temporary market closures, trading cancellations, and notifications to clearing and settlement institutions to postpone settlement to the China Securities Regulatory Commission. The Exchange shall promptly announce the aforementioned decisions, except where they affect the handling of abnormal situations, market order, or market stability. After the cause of a technical suspension or temporary market closure is eliminated, the Exchange may decide to resume trading and will announce such decisions.
8.4 Except for special circumstances recognized by the Exchange, if bond trading resumes on the same day after a technical suspension or temporary market closure, the orders already accepted by the trading system before the technical suspension or temporary market closure shall be valid.
8.5 The Exchange shall not be liable for civil damages for any losses caused by the measures taken by the Exchange in response to abnormal trading situations, except where the Exchange is grossly at fault.
8.6 Specific provisions for handling abnormal trading situations shall be separately stipulated by the Exchange.
8.7 If a bond trading participant is unable to send a transaction declaration to the trading system due to technical failure or other reasons, they may submit an emergency declaration to the Exchange through other auxiliary declaration methods recognized by the Exchange. If the Exchange confirms the transaction based on the emergency declaration, both parties to the transaction shall acknowledge the transaction result and fulfill their clearing and settlement obligations.
8.8 In the event of disputes arising from bond transactions, the parties may resolve them through negotiation. If negotiation fails, the disputes may be resolved through arbitration or judicial proceedings in accordance with relevant laws or agreements. In the event of transaction disputes between bond trading participants or between members and brokerage clients, the Exchange may provide necessary transaction data in accordance with relevant regulations. Members are obligated to coordinate and resolve any doubts a brokerage client may have regarding a transaction.
Chapter Nine: Regulatory Measures and Disciplinary Actions
9.1 The Exchange may take the following routine work measures, individually or in combination, against bond trading participants, other bond investors, and institutions and their relevant personnel that provide professional services for bond trading, and the relevant institutions and personnel shall cooperate: (i) requesting explanations and clarifications on relevant issues; (ii) requesting the provision of relevant documents or materials; (iii) meeting with relevant personnel; (iv) reviewing and examining transaction-related materials; (v) issuing recommendations for regulating transactions; (vi) informing relevant units of relevant situations; and (vii) other routine work measures.
9.2 If an investor violates laws, administrative regulations, departmental rules, normative documents, or the relevant business rules of this exchange in conducting bond transactions, or seriously disrupts the market trading order, this exchange may request the China Securities Regulatory Commission to investigate and deal with the matter.
9.3 If any participant in bond trading, institution providing professional services for bond trading, or related entity violates these rules or other relevant business rules of the Exchange, the Exchange may take the following self-regulatory measures against them, depending on the severity of the circumstances: (i) verbal warning; (ii) meeting and discussion; (iii) written warning; (iv) suspension of acceptance or processing of related business; (v) restriction of trading; (vi) other self-regulatory measures stipulated by the Exchange.
9.4 For serious violations among the key monitoring items listed in Article 7.1, the Exchange may take the following self-regulatory measures as appropriate: (i) verbal warning; (ii) written warning; (iii) meeting for discussion; (iv) requiring a written commitment; (v) including the securities account in the key monitoring accounts; (vi) suspending trading during the trading day; (vii) restricting trading after the market closes; (viii) other self-regulatory measures stipulated by the Exchange. If there is any objection to the measures in item (vii) above, an application for review may be submitted to the Exchange within 15 trading days from the date of receipt of the relevant decision or the date of public announcement of the relevant decision (whichever comes first). The relevant measures will not be suspended during the review period.
9.5 If a bond trading participant, other bond investor, or institution providing professional services for bond trading, and its related personnel, violate this rule or other relevant business rules of the Exchange, the Exchange may, depending on the severity of the circumstances, take the following disciplinary actions: (i) public criticism; (ii) public censure; (iii) collection of punitive liquidated damages; (iv) suspension or restriction of bond trading authority; (v) cancellation of bond trading authority; (vi) cancellation of bond trading participant qualification; (vii) other disciplinary actions stipulated by the Exchange. Before a relevant disciplinary decision is made, the party concerned may apply for a hearing in accordance with the scope and procedures for acceptance stipulated in the relevant business rules of the Exchange. If the party concerned is dissatisfied with the relevant disciplinary decision made by the Exchange, they may apply for a review in accordance with the scope and procedures for acceptance stipulated in the relevant business rules of the Exchange.
9.6 The Exchange will record the relevant self-regulatory measures and disciplinary actions taken against the parties involved in their integrity files, and may, as appropriate, notify the China Securities Regulatory Commission or its local offices, local governments and industry self-regulatory organizations, or publish them on the Exchange's website.
Chapter Ten Supplementary Provisions
10.1 Definitions of Terms Related to These Rules: (i) Benchmark Market Making Products: Bond products designated by the Exchange for market making, including key-term government bonds, corporate bonds, etc. (ii) Spot Bond Trading: A transaction in which both parties transfer ownership of securities at a certain price. (iii) Bond Repurchase Transactions: A transaction in which the borrower pledges or transfers bonds to the lender to obtain funds, repays the funds upon maturity, and simultaneously releases the pledge or repurchases the corresponding bonds. (iv) General Bond Pledged Repurchase Transactions: A transaction in which the borrower declares eligible bonds as collateral, uses the value of the pledged bonds calculated at the corresponding discount rate as the financing amount, and both parties agree to release the bond pledge upon repayment of funds at the end of the repurchase period. Pledged bonds refer to bonds used as collateral in general bond pledged repurchase transactions. Specific regulations for general bond pledged repurchase transactions will be formulated separately by the Exchange. (v) Bond Pre-Issuance Transactions: Bond trading activities conducted during a specific period before bond issuance and settled after bond issuance. (vi) Accrued Interest: Interest accrued on the bonds from the date of this interest accrual to the date of bond transfer. The date of bond transfer is determined according to the relevant rules of the registration and settlement institution. (vii) Face Value: Unless otherwise specified, the face value of the bonds refers to the face value at the time of issuance, and the face value of bond repurchase refers to the repurchase amount. (viii) Yuan: If denominated in RMB, it refers to RMB Yuan; if denominated in foreign currency, it refers to the foreign currency unit. (ix) Matching Reference Price: The virtual matching transaction price formed by all orders in the centralized order book according to the matching rules as of the disclosure date. (x) Matched Quantity: The virtual transaction quantity formed by all orders in the centralized order book according to the matching rules as of the disclosure date. (xi) Unmatched Quantity: The remaining quantity of buyer or seller orders in the centralized order book at the matching reference price that cannot be virtually matched according to the matching reference price as of the disclosure date.
10.2 Investors participating in bond trading on this exchange through relevant interconnectivity mechanisms shall follow the business rules and arrangements of this exchange and the relevant trading venues.
10.3 For other securities and trading instruments traded using the trading system and the trading methods stipulated in these rules, the relevant provisions of these rules shall apply by analogy. Unless otherwise stipulated by the Exchange.
10.4 Bond trading participants shall pay bond trading-related transaction handling fees and other expenses to the Exchange in accordance with regulations. The fee items, fee standards, and management methods shall be separately stipulated by the Exchange.
10.5 The time mentioned in these rules shall be based on the time of the trading system.
10.6 The terms “exceeding,” “higher than,” “lower than,” “better than,” and “insufficient” used in these rules do not include the number itself, while “reaching,” “above,” “above,” and “within” include the number itself.
10.7 This rule shall come into effect upon being reviewed and approved by the Board of Directors of this Exchange and approved by the China Securities Regulatory Commission, and the same applies to any amendments.
10.8 This rule is subject to interpretation by this firm.
10.9 This rule shall come into effect on April 25, 2022. After this rule comes into effect, any previous regulations of the Exchange concerning bond trading that are inconsistent with this rule shall be superseded by this rule.

Chapter 1 General Provisions
1.1 This rule is formulated in accordance with the Securities Law of the People's Republic of China, the Administrative Measures for Securities Exchanges, and other laws, administrative regulations, departmental rules, normative documents (hereinafter collectively referred to as laws and regulations) and the Articles of Association of the Shenzhen Stock Exchange in order to regulate securities market trading activities, maintain securities market order, and protect the legitimate rights and interests of investors.
1.2 This rule applies to the trading of stocks, depositary receipts, funds, warrants, and other trading instruments approved by the China Securities Regulatory Commission (hereinafter referred to as "securities") listed on the Shenzhen Stock Exchange (hereinafter referred to as "the Exchange"). Trading of depositary receipts on the Main Board shall be governed by the relevant regulations for Main Board A-share trading, and trading of depositary receipts on the ChiNext Board shall be governed by the relevant regulations for ChiNext Board A-share trading. Where the Exchange has separate regulations for trading depositary receipts, preferred shares, publicly offered infrastructure investment funds, etc., those regulations shall prevail.
1.3 Securities trading shall adhere to the principles of openness, fairness, and impartiality.
1.4 Securities transactions shall comply with laws and regulations, these rules and other relevant regulations of the Exchange, and shall adhere to the principles of voluntariness, compensation and good faith.
1.5 Securities trading shall be conducted through paperless centralized trading or other methods approved by the China Securities Regulatory Commission.
Chapter Two: Trading Markets

Section 1 Trading Venues and Commodities
2.1.1 This exchange provides trading venues and facilities for securities trading. The trading venues and facilities consist of trading mainframes, trading units, order placement systems, and related communication systems.
2.1.2 The following securities may be listed and traded on the Exchange: (i) stocks; (ii) depositary receipts; (iii) funds; (iv) warrants; and (v) other trading instruments approved by the China Securities Regulatory Commission.
Section Two: Trading Participants
2.2.1 Members and institutions recognized by the Exchange shall apply to the Exchange for trading authority to enter the Exchange's market and become trading participants. Trading participants shall conduct securities trading through trading units opened with the Exchange and comply with the relevant provisions of these rules and other business rules of the Exchange regarding securities trading.
2.2.2 A trading unit refers to the basic business unit established by a trading participant upon application to the Exchange to participate in securities trading on the Exchange and to accept the Exchange's supervision and services.
2.2.3 The specific provisions regarding trading units and trading permissions shall be formulated separately by the Exchange.
Third Trading Session
2.3.1 The trading days of this exchange are Monday to Friday. The exchange market is closed on national statutory holidays and holidays announced by the exchange.
2.3.2 For securities traded via competitive bidding, the opening call auction period is from 9:15 to 9:25 each trading day; the continuous bidding period is from 9:30 to 11:30 and from 13:00 to 14:57; and the closing call auction period is from 14:57 to 15:00. With the approval of the China Securities Regulatory Commission (CSRC), the Exchange may adjust the trading hours.
2.3.3 If the market is suspended during trading hours for any reason, the trading hours will not be extended.
Chapter Three: Securities Trading

Section 1 General Provisions
3.1.1 After accepting an investor's buy or sell order, a member shall verify that the investor possesses the corresponding securities or funds, and shall report to the exchange in accordance with the content of the order, assuming corresponding transaction and settlement responsibilities. If a member accepts an investor's buy or sell order and a transaction is completed, the investor shall deliver to the member the securities they entrusted the member to sell or the proceeds from the securities they entrusted the member to purchase, and the member shall deliver to the investor the proceeds from the sale of securities or the securities purchased.
3.1.2 Trading participants send buy and sell orders to the exchange's trading host through the order system and complete transactions in accordance with these rules. The transaction records are sent to the trading participants by the exchange.
3.1.3 Transaction participants shall properly keep the entrustment and declaration records in accordance with relevant regulations.
3.1.4 Securities purchased by investors may not be sold before settlement, except for those subject to day trading. Day trading refers to the sale of all or part of securities purchased by investors after the transaction has been confirmed, before settlement is completed.
3.1.5 The following securities are subject to same-day turnover trading: (i) bond exchange-traded funds (ETFs); (ii) listed money market funds; (iii) gold ETFs; (iv) cross-border ETFs and cross-border listed ETFs; and (v) commodity futures ETFs. The cross-border ETFs and cross-border listed ETFs mentioned above are limited to open-ended funds whose tracked index constituent securities or investment targets are subject to same-day turnover trading. B shares are subject to turnover trading starting from the next trading day. With the approval of the China Securities Regulatory Commission (CSRC), the Exchange may adjust the types of securities subject to turnover trading and the turnover method.
3.1.6 The Exchange may implement a prime dealer system as needed by the market. Specific regulations will be formulated separately by the Exchange and will take effect after being approved by the China Securities Regulatory Commission.
3.1.7 Investors participating in securities trading or related business on this exchange should fully understand and be aware of the relevant risks, laws and regulations, and the exchange's business rules, comply with the relevant requirements for investor suitability management, and prudently judge whether to participate in securities trading or related business based on their own risk awareness and tolerance. Members should earnestly fulfill their investor suitability management obligations, fully disclose investment risks, and guide clients to invest rationally.
3.1.8 Any programmatic trading that generates or issues trading instructions automatically through computer programs shall comply with the regulations of the China Securities Regulatory Commission (CSRC) and report to the Exchange, and shall not affect the security of the Exchange's system or the normal trading order.
Section Two: Entrustment
3.2.1 Investors buying and selling securities shall open securities accounts and funds accounts in their real names and sign a securities trading agency agreement with the member. After the agreement takes effect, the investor becomes a client of the member's brokerage business. Investors shall open securities accounts in accordance with the regulations of the securities registration and settlement institution.
3.2.2 Investors may entrust members to buy or sell securities through written, telephone, self-service terminals, internet, or other self-service methods. When investors participate in securities trading through self-service methods, members shall sign a self-service entrustment agreement with them.
3.2.3 Investors who conduct self-service transactions via telephone, self-service terminals, the Internet, or other means shall operate in accordance with relevant regulations. Members shall record information such as the investor's telephone number, network card address, and IP address.
3.2.4 Unless otherwise stipulated by the Exchange, an investor's order shall include: (i) securities account number; (ii) securities code; (iii) buy/sell direction; (iv) order quantity; (v) order price; (vi) order type; and (vii) other contents required by the Exchange and its members.
3.2.5 Investors may instruct members to buy or sell securities using limit orders or market orders. A limit order is an order from an investor to a member to buy or sell securities at a specified price; the member must submit a buy order at or below the specified price and a sell order at or above the specified price. A market order is an order from an investor to a member to buy or sell securities at the market price.
3.2.6 Investors may cancel the unexecuted portion of their orders.
3.2.7 Upon confirmation of a revoked or invalidated entrustment, the member shall promptly return the corresponding funds or securities to the investor.
Section 3 Application
3.3.1 The Exchange accepts bid submissions from trading participants from 9:15 to 9:25, 9:30 to 11:30, and 13:00 to 15:00 each trading day. From 9:20 to 9:25 and from 14:57 to 15:00 each trading day, the Exchange's trading system does not accept cancellations of bid submissions; during other submission periods, unexecuted bids can be cancelled. The Exchange may adjust the submission acceptance times.
3.3.2 Members shall promptly submit their orders to the Exchange in the order in which they receive investor mandates. Orders and cancellations are valid only after confirmation by the Exchange's trading system.
3.3.3 When buying or selling securities through competitive bidding, the Exchange accepts limit orders and market orders from trading participants.
3.3.4 The Exchange may accept the following types of market orders: (i) Best price order against the counterparty; (ii) Best price order against the counterparty; (iii) Best five-level immediate execution with remaining cancellation order; (iv) Immediate execution with remaining cancellation order; (v) Full execution or cancellation order; (vi) Other types specified by the Exchange. For a best price order against the counterparty, the order price is the best price in the counterparty queue of the centralized order book when the order is entered into the trading system. For a best price order against the counterparty, the order price is the best price in the counterparty queue of the centralized order book when the order is entered into the trading system. For a best five-level immediate execution with remaining cancellation order, the order price is the counterparty price, and the order is executed sequentially with the best five price levels of the counterparty order queue in the centralized order book when the order is entered into the trading system; any unexecuted portion is automatically cancelled. For an immediate execution with remaining cancellation order, the order price is the counterparty price, and the order is executed sequentially with all counterparty order queues in the centralized order book when the order is entered into the trading system; any unexecuted portion is automatically cancelled. For a full execution or cancellation of an order, the execution price is the counterparty's price. If the order can be fully executed by sequentially matching all the counterparty's order queues in the centralized order book when it enters the trading system, then the order will be executed sequentially; otherwise, the order will be automatically cancelled.
3.3.5 Market orders are only applicable to transactions during continuous trading hours for securities with price limits. At other trading hours, the trading system does not accept market orders.
3.3.6 When a best-price order is entered into the trading system, if there is no corresponding order in the order book, the order will be automatically cancelled. When other market order types are entered into the trading system, if there is no corresponding order in the order book, the order will be automatically cancelled.
3.3.7 Limit orders shall include the securities account number, securities code, trading unit code, securities brokerage identification code, buy/sell direction, quantity, and price. Market orders shall include the order type, securities account number, securities code, trading unit code, securities brokerage identification code, buy/sell direction, and quantity. Orders shall be transmitted in accordance with the format prescribed by the Exchange. The Exchange may adjust the content of orders as needed by the market.
3.3.8 When purchasing securities through competitive bidding, the order quantity shall be 100 shares (units) or an integral multiple thereof. When selling securities, any remaining balance less than 100 shares (units) shall be sold in a single order.
3.3.9 The number of shares (units) submitted in a single securities auction transaction shall not exceed 1 million shares. For ChiNext stocks, the number of shares submitted in a single limit order shall not exceed 300,000 shares, and the number of shares submitted in a single market order shall not exceed 150,000 shares.
3.3.10 The pricing unit for stock transactions is "price per share", the pricing unit for depositary receipts is "price per depositary receipt", and the pricing unit for fund transactions is "price per fund unit".
3.3.11 The minimum price fluctuation unit for A-share trading is RMB 0.01; the minimum price fluctuation unit for fund trading is RMB 0.001; and the minimum price fluctuation unit for B-share trading is HKD 0.01. The Exchange may implement different minimum price fluctuation units based on the share price level.
3.3.12 The Exchange may adjust the minimum increment for the quantity and price of a single securities transaction as needed by the market.
3.3.13 The Exchange imposes price fluctuation limits on securities trading. The price fluctuation limit for main board stocks is 10%, and the price fluctuation limit for ChiNext stocks is 20%. The price fluctuation limit for funds is 10%. For index ETFs and LOFs tracking indices whose constituent stocks are solely ChiNext stocks or other stocks subject to a 20% price fluctuation limit, and for LOFs whose fund contracts stipulate that at least 80% of their non-cash fund assets are invested in ChiNext stocks or other stocks subject to a 20% price fluctuation limit, the price fluctuation limit is 20%. Exceptions are made by the Exchange. The Exchange publishes a list of funds with a 20% price fluctuation limit to the market.
3.3.14 The formula for calculating the price limit is: Price limit = Previous closing price × (1 ± Price limit ratio).
3.3.15 Stocks shall not be subject to price fluctuation limits under any of the following circumstances: (i) the first five trading days after the initial public offering; (ii) the first day of relisting; (iii) the first day of entering the delisting period; (iv) other circumstances as determined by the Exchange.
3.3.16 The valid bid prices for limit orders during the continuous trading phase of stocks shall comply with the following provisions: (i) the bid price shall not exceed 102% of the bid benchmark price and the higher of the ten smallest bid price increments above the bid benchmark price; (ii) the bid price shall not be lower than 98% of the sell benchmark price and the lower of the ten smallest bid price increments below the sell benchmark price. The bid (sell) benchmark price is the lowest sell (highest buy) bid price disclosed in real time; if there is no lowest sell (highest buy) bid price disclosed in real time, it is the highest buy (lowest sell) bid price disclosed in real time; if there is no highest buy (lowest sell) bid price disclosed in real time, it is the most recent transaction price; if there is no transaction on the day, it is the previous closing price. The provisions of the preceding two paragraphs do not apply to limit orders during the temporary suspension phase of trading during the market opening period.
3.3.17 For stocks without price fluctuation limits, the valid bid price range during the opening call auction is within 900% of the previous closing price displayed in the real-time market data. During the intraday temporary suspension and closing call auction, the valid bid price range is within 10% above and below the most recent transaction price. The closing call auction will match bids within the valid bid price range.
3.3.18 Declarations exceeding the price limit or the valid price range are invalid.
3.3.19 The calculation results of the price fluctuation limit and the valid bid/ask price range are rounded to the minimum bid/ask price increment. If the absolute value of the difference between the price fluctuation limit and the previous closing price, or the difference between the upper or lower limit of the valid bid/ask price range and the benchmark price, is lower than the minimum bid/ask price increment, the corresponding price is calculated by adding or subtracting one minimum bid/ask price increment from the previous closing price or the benchmark price. If the price fluctuation limit and the upper or lower limit of the valid bid/ask price range are lower than the minimum bid/ask price increment, the minimum bid/ask price increment is used as the corresponding price.
3.3.20 With the approval of the China Securities Regulatory Commission, the Exchange may adjust the price fluctuation limits and the range of valid bid prices for securities.
3.3.21 Valid only on the day of submission. If a bid cannot be fully executed at once, the unexecuted portion will continue to participate in the auction that day, except for market bid types in items 3 to 5 of Article 3.3.4.
Section 4 Bidding and Transaction
3.4.1 Securities auction trading adopts two methods: call auction and continuous auction. Call auction refers to an auction method in which buy and sell orders received within a certain period are matched all at once. Continuous auction refers to an auction method in which buy and sell orders are matched one by one.
3.4.2 Securities auction trading is matched according to the principles of price priority and time priority. The price priority principle is: higher-priced buy orders take precedence over lower-priced buy orders, and lower-priced sell orders take precedence over higher-priced sell orders. The time priority principle is: for buy and sell orders with the same direction and price, those submitted earlier take precedence over those submitted later. The order is determined by the time the trading system accepts the orders.
3.4.3 During the call auction, the principle for determining the transaction price is as follows: (i) it can achieve the maximum trading volume; (ii) all buy orders above the price and all sell orders below the price are executed; (iii) at least one side of the buyers or sellers at the same price is fully executed. If two or more prices meet the above conditions, the price with the smallest difference between the cumulative number of buy orders above the price and the cumulative number of sell orders below the price shall be the transaction price; if the difference between the cumulative number of buy and sell orders is still equal, the price closest to the previous closing price displayed in the real-time market data shall be the transaction price during the opening call auction, and the price closest to the most recent transaction price shall be the transaction price during the intraday and closing call auctions. All transactions in the call auction shall be executed at the same price.
3.4.4 During continuous bidding, the principle for determining the transaction price is as follows: (i) if the highest bid price is the same as the lowest ask price, the transaction price shall be the same; (ii) if the bid price is higher than the lowest ask price in the centralized order book at that time, the transaction price shall be the lowest ask price in the centralized order book at that time; (iii) if the ask price is lower than the highest bid price in the centralized order book at that time, the transaction price shall be the highest bid price in the centralized order book at that time.
3.4.5 A trade is established once a buy or sell order is matched and executed by the trading system. Trades that comply with all provisions of these rules become effective upon establishment, and both the buyer and seller must acknowledge the trade result and fulfill their clearing and settlement obligations. The Exchange may take appropriate measures or declare invalid trades that result in serious consequences due to force majeure, unforeseen events, or unauthorized access to the trading system. The Exchange may take appropriate measures against obviously unfair trades upon its determination. The Exchange reserves the right to cancel trades that violate these rules and seriously disrupt the normal operation of the securities market. Any losses incurred as a result shall be borne by the violating trader.
3.4.6 For transactions completed in accordance with these rules, the transaction results shall be based on the transaction data recorded by the trading system.
3.4.7 The clearing and settlement of securities transactions shall be handled in accordance with the regulations of the securities registration and settlement institution.
Section 5 Bulk Transactions
3.5.1 Block trades may be used for securities transactions conducted on this Exchange if the following conditions are met: (i) A-shares: a single transaction of no less than 300,000 shares or a transaction amount of no less than RMB 2 million; (ii) B-shares: a single transaction of no less than 30,000 shares or a transaction amount of no less than HKD 200,000; (iii) Funds: a single transaction of no less than 2 million units or a transaction amount of no less than RMB 2 million. This Exchange may adjust the minimum block trade limit as needed by the market.
3.5.2 This exchange adopts two methods for block trades: negotiated block trades and after-hours pricing block trades. Negotiated block trades refer to a trading method where both parties designate each other as counterparties and negotiate to determine the transaction price and quantity. After-hours pricing block trades refer to a trading method where, after the close of securities trading, buy and sell orders for block trades are matched continuously on a time-priority basis using the closing price of the security on that day or the volume-weighted average price of the security on that day.
3.5.3 For block trades conducted via negotiated agreements, the Exchange accepts orders from 9:15 to 11:30 and from 13:00 to 15:30 each trading day. For block trades conducted via after-hours pricing, the Exchange accepts orders from 15:05 to 15:30 each trading day. The Exchange will not accept negotiated block trade orders for securities that are suspended from trading for the entire day, are under temporary suspension, or are suspended until the market closes. The Exchange will not accept after-hours pricing block trade orders for securities that are suspended from trading for the entire day or are suspended until the market closes.
3.5.4 For block trades of securities with price fluctuation limits, the bid price shall be determined within the daily price fluctuation limit range for that security. For block trades of securities without price fluctuation limits, the bid price shall not exceed the lower of 120% of the average real-time transaction price of the security in the daily auction trading and the highest transaction price, and shall not be lower than the higher of 80% of the average real-time transaction price of the security in the daily auction trading and the lowest transaction price. The average price is calculated as follows: Average Price = Transaction Amount / Transaction Count. The result is rounded to the minimum price fluctuation unit.
3.5.5 The Exchange accepts the following types of block trade declarations: (i) declaration of intent; (ii) declaration of transaction; (iii) declaration of price; and (iv) other declarations.
3.5.6 The intention declaration instruction for a block trade under a negotiated agreement shall include the securities account number, securities code, buy/sell direction, and the trading unit code of the party making the declaration. An intention declaration does not imply an obligation to execute a trade and can be withdrawn. The execution declaration instruction for a block trade under a negotiated agreement shall include the securities account number, securities code, buy/sell direction, price, quantity, the trading unit codes of both the party making the declaration and the counterparty, and the agreement number. Execution declarations must explicitly specify the price and quantity. The Exchange will confirm executions for execution declarations where all elements such as the agreement number, securities code, buy/sell direction, price, and quantity match. Declarations can be withdrawn before execution confirmation. The pricing declaration instruction for a block trade under a negotiated agreement shall include the securities account number, securities code, buy/sell direction, price, quantity, and the trading unit code of the party making the declaration. All market participants can submit execution declarations, and all or part of the trade can be executed at the specified price and pricing declaration. The Exchange will confirm executions according to time priority. Unexecuted portions of pricing declarations can be withdrawn. The quantity or transaction amount of each executed pricing declaration must meet the minimum limit requirement for block trades under a negotiated agreement.
3.5.7 The confirmation time for block trades under securities agreements is from 15:00 to 15:30 on each trading day.
3.5.8 The order for after-hours pricing block trades shall include the securities account number, securities code, trading unit code, securities brokerage identification code, buy/sell direction, quantity, and price type. The price types for after-hours pricing block trades include: (i) the closing price of the security on the current day; and (ii) the volume-weighted average price of the security on the current day. Unexecuted orders may be cancelled within the order acceptance period.
3.5.9 During trading hours, the Exchange will publish transaction information for after-hours block trades in real time through the trading system. The information includes: security code, security abbreviation, price, total daily trading volume, total daily trading amount, and the number of buy or sell orders submitted in real time.
3.5.10 After the close of trading each day, the Exchange will publish the following trading information on its website: (i) information on each block trade, including: security code, security abbreviation, trading volume, trading price, and the names of the member securities business departments or trading units of the buyer and seller; (ii) the cumulative trading volume and cumulative trading amount of a single security's after-hours pricing block trade, and the names of the five member securities business departments or trading units with the largest buy and sell amounts on that day, and their respective buy and sell amounts; (iii) the cumulative trading volume and cumulative trading amount of a single security's block trade, and the names of the five member securities business departments or trading units with the largest buy and sell amounts on that day, and their respective buy and sell amounts.
3.5.11 Block trades are not included in the calculation of the exchange's real-time market data and indices. The trading volume of a block trade is included in the total trading volume of the security on that day after the block trade is completed.
3.5.12 Members shall ensure that participants in block trades actually possess the securities or funds corresponding to the trade declaration.
Section 6. After-hours Fixed-Price Trading on the ChiNext Board
3.6.1 After-hours fixed-price trading on the ChiNext board refers to a trading method in which buy and sell orders at fixed prices are matched continuously, one by one, based on the principle of time priority after the close of trading on the ChiNext board and the closing price of the day.
3.6.2 The after-hours fixed-price trading period is from 15:05 to 15:30 each trading day. The submission period for after-hours fixed-price trading is from 9:15 to 11:30 and from 13:00 to 15:30 each trading day. If trading is suspended during the market opening period, submissions can continue during the suspension period. If trading is still suspended at 15:00 on the same day, after-hours fixed-price trading will not be conducted. Unexecuted submissions can be cancelled during the submission period. Cancellation instructions are valid only after confirmation by the exchange's trading system. After-hours fixed-price submissions are valid only for the day of submission.
3.6.3 Investors who buy or sell ChiNext stocks through after-hours fixed-price trading shall submit after-hours fixed-price order instructions to the member. After-hours fixed-price order instructions shall include the securities account number, securities code, buy/sell direction, limit price, and order quantity.
3.6.4 After-hours fixed-price order submissions from members and other trading participants shall include the securities account number, securities code, trading unit code, securities brokerage identification code, buy/sell direction, limit price, and quantity. After-hours fixed-price order submissions for Shenzhen-Hong Kong Stock Connect shall include the brokerage client code, securities code, brokerage code, buy/sell direction, limit price, and quantity.
3.6.5 After-hours fixed-price orders with a buy limit price lower than the closing price or a sell limit price higher than the closing price are invalid.
3.6.6 When buying ChiNext stocks through after-hours fixed-price trading, the order quantity must be 100 shares or an integral multiple thereof. When selling stocks, any remaining balance less than 100 shares must be sold in a single order. The quantity of a single after-hours fixed-price order shall not exceed 1 million shares.
3.6.7 During the after-hours fixed-price trading period, the exchange uses the closing price as the transaction price and performs continuous matching of after-hours fixed-price orders according to the time priority principle.
3.6.8 From 9:15 to 15:05 each trading day, after-hours fixed-price orders are not included in real-time market data; from 15:05 to 15:30, after-hours fixed-price orders and transactions are included in real-time market data. Real-time market data includes: security code, closing price, cumulative trading volume of after-hours fixed-price transactions for the day, cumulative trading value of after-hours fixed-price transactions for the day, and the number of real-time unexecuted buy or sell orders.
3.6.9 The trading volume and value of after-hours fixed-price transactions are included in the total trading volume and total trading value of the stock on that day after the end of the after-hours fixed-price trading period.
3.6.10 Shares sold through after-hours fixed-price trading shall be deemed to have been sold through competitive bidding.
3.6.11 After-hours fixed-price trading is subject to the Shenzhen-Hong Kong Stock Connect quota control. If the daily quota is exhausted during the after-hours fixed-price trading phase on the Hong Kong Stock Exchange, the Stock Exchange Securities Trading Service Company will cease accepting subsequent buy orders for the day, but will still accept sell orders. Once buy orders are suspended, they will not be resumed on the same day, unless otherwise stipulated by the Exchange.
Section 7 Margin Trading
3.7.1 Margin trading refers to the act of investors providing collateral to members to borrow funds to buy securities or borrowing securities and selling them.
3.7.2 Members participating in margin trading on this exchange shall apply to this exchange for margin trading authorization and conduct such transactions through the dedicated margin trading unit.
3.7.3 Investors engaging in margin trading shall open a margin securities account in accordance with regulations. The opening and closing of margin securities accounts shall be handled in accordance with the relevant regulations of members and securities registration and settlement institutions.
3.7.4 The Exchange makes the following provisions regarding margin trading: (i) trading procedures; (ii) securities that can be used for margin buying and short selling; (iii) the types of securities that can be used as collateral and the maximum discount rate; (iv) the maximum term of margin trading; (v) the initial margin ratio and the minimum maintenance margin ratio; (vi) the information disclosure and reporting system; (vii) market risk control measures; and (viii) other matters.
3.7.5 Before providing financing or securities lending to investors, members shall sign a financing and securities lending contract with them, explain the rules and contract contents of the financing and securities lending business to them, and require them to sign a risk disclosure statement.
3.7.6 If any abnormalities occur in margin trading activities that have already or may endanger market stability, the Exchange may, when it deems it necessary, suspend all or part of the margin trading of securities and issue a public announcement.
3.7.7 The specific rules for margin trading and securities lending shall be formulated separately by the Exchange and shall take effect after being approved by the China Securities Regulatory Commission.
Chapter Four Other Transaction Matters

Section 1 Sub-trusteeship
4.1.1 Investors may purchase securities through the same securities account at different securities branches of one or more members.
4.1.2 Securities purchased by investors can be sold through the original trading unit where the securities were purchased, or investors can issue a transfer order to the original trading unit and then sell the securities through the new trading unit. Specific regulations regarding transfer of custody are to be formulated by the securities registration and settlement institution.
Section 2 Opening Price and Closing Price
4.2.1 The opening price of a security is the price of the first transaction of that security on that day.
4.2.2 The opening price of a security is determined through a call auction; if it cannot be determined through a call auction, it is determined through continuous auction.
4.2.3 Unless otherwise stipulated in these rules, the closing price of a security shall be determined through a closing auction. If the closing auction fails to produce a closing price or no closing auction is conducted, the closing price shall be the volume-weighted average price of all transactions in the minute preceding the last transaction of the day (including the last transaction). If there are no transactions on a given day, the previous closing price shall be used as the closing price for that day.
Section 3 Listing, Delisting, Suspension and Resumption of Trading
4.3.1 The Exchange will conduct trading on listed securities.
4.3.2 If a security's listing period expires or it no longer meets the listing requirements by law, the Exchange will terminate its listing and delist it.
4.3.3 If any abnormal trading behavior or situation as stipulated in Article 6.1 occurs in securities trading, the Exchange may, as appropriate, suspend trading of the relevant securities, issue an announcement, and publish relevant transaction, share, and fund unit statistics as needed. Parties with disclosure obligations shall promptly issue announcements as required by the Exchange. The specific suspension and resumption times shall be subject to the relevant announcements.
4.3.4 For stocks without price fluctuation limits, the Exchange will implement intraday temporary suspensions under any of the following circumstances, with each temporary suspension lasting 10 minutes: (i) the intraday trading price rises or falls by 30% or more from the opening price of the day; (ii) the intraday trading price rises or falls by 60% or more from the opening price of the day; (iii) other circumstances determined by the China Securities Regulatory Commission (CSRC) or the Exchange. The specific time of the intraday temporary suspension will be subject to the Exchange's announcement. If the temporary suspension time extends beyond 14:57, trading will resume at 14:57, and a resumption call auction will be held for accepted orders, followed by a closing call auction. The Exchange may adjust relevant indicator thresholds or take further intraday risk control measures depending on the intraday trading situation.
4.3.5 When a security is suspended from trading, the market data released by the exchange includes information about that security; after a security is delisted, the market data will no longer include information about that security.
4.3.6 If a security is suspended from trading before 9:25 AM, an opening call auction will be held for accepted orders upon resumption of trading on the same day, and trading will continue for the remainder of the day. If a security is temporarily suspended from trading at 9:30 AM or later, an intraday call auction will be held for accepted orders upon resumption of trading on the same day, and trading will continue for the remainder of the day. During the suspension period, orders can be submitted and cancelled. The call auction reference price, matched volume, and unmatched volume will not be disclosed during the suspension period.
4.3.7 The listing, delisting, suspension and resumption of trading of securities shall be announced by the Exchange or the securities issuer.
4.3.8 Other provisions regarding the listing, delisting, suspension and resumption of trading of securities shall be implemented in accordance with the listing rules of this exchange and other relevant regulations.
Section 4 Ex-rights and Ex-dividend
4.4.1 When listed securities undergo rights distribution, capitalization of reserves, rights issues, or other similar events, the Exchange shall process the ex-rights and ex-dividend treatment of the securities on the trading day following the record date (the last trading day for B shares), unless otherwise stipulated by the Exchange.
4.4.2 The formula for calculating the ex-rights (ex-dividend) reference price is: Ex-rights (ex-dividend) reference price = [(previous closing price - cash dividend) + rights issue price × share change ratio] ÷ (1 + share change ratio). If the securities issuer deems it necessary to adjust the above calculation formula, it may submit an adjustment application to the Exchange and explain the reasons. With the Exchange's approval, the securities issuer shall publish the applicable ex-rights (ex-dividend) reference price calculation formula to the market.
4.4.3 For securities trading on the ex-rights (ex-dividend) date, the ex-rights (ex-dividend) reference price shall be used as the benchmark for calculating the price fluctuation range, unless otherwise stipulated by the Exchange.
Section 5 Risk Warning Board Trading Matters
4.5.1 Stocks subject to risk warning in accordance with the listing rules of this exchange (hereinafter referred to as risk warning stocks) and stocks for which the exchange has made a decision to terminate listing but are in the delisting period and have not yet been delisted (hereinafter referred to as delisting stocks) are traded on the risk warning board, and their trading information is independent of the trading information of other stocks and is disclosed separately.
4.5.2 When ordinary investors purchase risk-alert stocks or delisted stocks for the first time, they shall sign a risk disclosure statement for the risk-alert stock or a risk disclosure statement for the delisted stock, either in paper or electronic form. Members shall not accept purchase orders from ordinary investors who have not signed the risk disclosure statement.
4.5.3 Individual investors participating in the purchase and trading of delisted stocks must have more than two years of stock trading experience, and the average daily assets in their securities account and cash account must be no less than RMB 500,000 for the 20 trading days prior to applying for the trading permission, excluding securities and funds borrowed by the investor through margin trading.
4.5.4 The total number of shares of a single risk-alert stock purchased by an investor on a given day through competitive bidding, block trading, and after-hours fixed-price trading shall not exceed 500,000 shares, unless otherwise stipulated by the Exchange. Members shall take effective measures to monitor the total number of single risk-alert stocks purchased by investors from that member on a given day; if any violation of the preceding paragraph is discovered, a warning and prevention shall be issued, and the matter shall be reported to the Exchange in a timely manner.
4.5.5 The Exchange imposes price fluctuation limits on stocks under risk alert and stocks undergoing delisting. The price fluctuation limit for stocks under risk alert on the main board is 5%, and for stocks undergoing delisting, it is 10%. The price fluctuation limit for stocks under risk alert and stocks undergoing delisting on the ChiNext board is 20%. No price fluctuation limits apply on the first day of the delisting period. With the approval of the China Securities Regulatory Commission (CSRC), the Exchange may adjust the price fluctuation limits for stocks under risk alert and stocks undergoing delisting.
4.5.6 During the delisting period, the Exchange shall publish the names of the five member securities business departments or trading units with the largest buy and sell amounts on that day, as well as their respective buy and sell amounts.
4.5.7 Trading during the delisting period is not included in the calculation of public securities information disclosure, abnormal fluctuation indicators, and severe abnormal fluctuation indicators.
4.5.8 Trading during the delisting period of a stock is not included in the calculation of the exchange's indices, but the trading volume is included in the total market trading volume for that day.
Chapter Five Transaction Information

Section 1 General Provisions
5.1.1 The Exchange publishes real-time securities trading quotes, securities indices, and other trading information, including public securities trading information, on each trading day.
5.1.2 The Exchange shall promptly compile various daily, weekly, monthly and annual reports reflecting market transaction conditions and publish them through the Exchange's website or other media.
5.1.3 All trading information belongs to the Exchange. No organization or individual may use or disseminate it without permission. Organizations and individuals authorized by the Exchange to use trading information may not provide such information to other organizations or individuals for use or disseminate it without consent. The management regulations for securities trading information will be formulated separately by the Exchange.
Section 2 Real-time Market Data
5.2.1 During the opening and closing call auctions, real-time market data includes: security code, security abbreviation, call auction reference price, matched volume, and unmatched volume.
5.2.2 During continuous bidding, real-time market data includes: security code, security abbreviation, previous closing price, most recent transaction price, highest price of the day, lowest price of the day, cumulative trading volume of the day, cumulative trading amount of the day, the highest five buy orders and quantities in real time, and the lowest five sell orders and quantities in real time.
5.2.3 The closing price displayed in real-time market data is the closing price of the security on the previous trading day, except in the following circumstances: (i) On the first day of listing of shares issued through an initial public offering, the closing price displayed in real-time market data is the issue price; (ii) On the first day of relisting, the closing price displayed in real-time market data is the closing price of the company's shares on the last trading day of the National Equities Exchange and Quotations (NEEQ) or other securities trading venues, unless otherwise stipulated by the Exchange; (iii) On the first day of listing of fund units, the closing price displayed in real-time market data is the net asset value per unit of the fund recently announced by the fund manager, unless otherwise stipulated by the Exchange; (iv) On the ex-rights (ex-dividend) date of a security, the closing price displayed in real-time market data is the ex-rights (ex-dividend) reference price of the security; (v) Other circumstances stipulated by the Exchange.
5.2.4 Real-time market data is transmitted through the Exchange’s licensed communication system, and trading participants shall use it within the scope permitted by the Exchange.
5.2.5 This exchange may adjust the method and content of real-time market information releases according to market needs.
Section 3 Securities Indices
5.3.1 This exchange compiles securities indices such as composite indices, component indices, and sector indices to reflect the changes and trends in the overall price of securities or the price of a certain type of securities, and releases market information in real time.
5.3.2 The method for setting up and compiling securities indices shall be formulated separately by the Exchange.
Section 4 Public Information on Securities Auction Transactions
5.4.1 If any of the following situations occur in the competitive bidding trading of stocks and closed-end funds with price fluctuation limits, the Exchange shall publish the names of the five member securities business departments or trading units with the largest buy and sell amounts of the relevant securities on that day, and their respective buy and sell amounts: (i) The top five main board stocks and closed-end funds with a daily closing price fluctuation deviation of ±7%, and the top five ChiNext stocks with a daily closing price fluctuation deviation of ±15%. The formula for calculating the closing price fluctuation deviation is: Closing price fluctuation deviation = Single security fluctuation - Corresponding category index fluctuation. If the security price reaches the fluctuation limit, the corresponding fluctuation limit ratio shall be used for calculation. (ii) The top five main board stocks and closed-end funds with a daily price fluctuation of 15%, and the top five ChiNext stocks with a daily price fluctuation of 30%. The formula for calculating price amplitude is: Price amplitude = (Highest price of the day - Lowest price of the day) / Lowest price of the day × 100% (III) The top five main board stocks and closed-end funds with a daily turnover rate of 20%, and the top five ChiNext stocks with a daily turnover rate of 30%. The formula for calculating turnover rate is: Turnover rate = Number of shares traded (shares) / Number of shares freely tradable (shares) × 100% If the closing price change deviation, closing price change, price amplitude, or turnover rate are the same, they are selected according to the transaction amount and transaction volume in that order. The corresponding classification indices for main board A-share stocks, ChiNext stocks, B-share stocks, and closed-end funds are the Shenzhen A-share Index, ChiNext Composite Index, Shenzhen B-share Index, and Shenzhen Lefu Fund Index compiled by this exchange.
5.4.2 For stocks without price fluctuation limits as stipulated in Article 3.3.15, the Exchange will publish the names of the five member securities business departments or trading units with the largest buy and sell amounts on that day, along with their respective buy and sell amounts. For stocks undergoing initial public offerings (IPOs), the above information will only be published on the first day of listing.
5.4.3 The following situations constitute abnormal fluctuations in the competitive bidding transactions of stocks and closed-end funds. The Exchange will publish the names of the five member securities business departments or trading units with the largest cumulative purchase and sale amounts during the period of abnormal fluctuations, as well as their respective cumulative purchase and sale amounts: (I) The cumulative deviation of the daily closing price of main board stocks and closed-end funds reaches ±20% within three consecutive trading days, and the cumulative deviation of the daily closing price of ChiNext stocks reaches ±30% within three consecutive trading days. The formula for calculating the cumulative deviation of closing price is: Cumulative deviation of closing price = (closing price at the end of the period / closing price before the beginning of the period - 1) × 100% - (closing points at the end of the period of the corresponding index / closing points before the beginning of the period - 1) × 100%. If the securities have undergone ex-rights or ex-dividend during the period, the closing price will be adjusted accordingly. (II) The cumulative deviation of the daily closing price of ST and *ST main board stocks reaches ±12% within three consecutive trading days. (iii) The ratio of the average daily turnover rate of main board stocks and closed-end funds over three consecutive trading days to the average daily turnover rate of the previous five trading days reaches 30 times, and the cumulative turnover rate of the security over three consecutive trading days reaches 20%. (iv) Other circumstances deemed abnormal fluctuations by the China Securities Regulatory Commission or the Exchange.
5.4.4 Any of the following situations in stock auction trading constitutes a serious abnormal fluctuation, and the Exchange will publish investor classification and trading statistics during the period of serious abnormal fluctuation: (i) Main board stocks experience four instances of the same-direction abnormal fluctuation as stipulated in items (i) and (ii) of Article 5.4.3 within ten consecutive trading days, and ChiNext stocks experience three instances of the same-direction abnormal fluctuation as stipulated in Article 5.4.3 within ten consecutive trading days; (ii) The cumulative deviation of the daily closing price change reaches +100% (-50%) within ten consecutive trading days; (iii) The cumulative deviation of the daily closing price change reaches +200% (-70%) within thirty consecutive trading days; (iv) Other situations deemed serious abnormal fluctuations by the China Securities Regulatory Commission (CSRC) or the Exchange. If multiple situations of serious abnormal fluctuations occur in stock trading, the Exchange will publish them together.
5.4.5 The Exchange may adjust the criteria for identifying abnormal fluctuations and severe abnormal fluctuations based on market conditions. The abnormal fluctuation index and severe abnormal fluctuation index shall be recalculated from the next trading day following the Exchange's announcement or the date of resumption of trading. Stocks without price fluctuation limits as stipulated in Article 3.3.15 shall not be included in the calculation of the abnormal fluctuation index and severe abnormal fluctuation index.
5.4.6 In the event of severe abnormal fluctuations in stock trading, listed companies shall promptly investigate and take corresponding measures in accordance with the listing rules. If, after investigation, the listed company finds no undisclosed material matters that should be disclosed and is unable to provide a reasonable explanation for the abnormal fluctuations, in addition to handling the matter in accordance with the listing rules, the Exchange may, based on market conditions, strengthen the monitoring of abnormal trading and require members to take effective measures to alert clients to the risks.
5.4.7 If public information on securities transactions involves institutional trading units, the published name shall be "Institutional Unit".
Chapter Six: Supervision of Securities Transactions
6.1 The Exchange will focus on monitoring the following matters in securities trading: (i) suspected insider trading, trading on undisclosed information, market manipulation and other illegal and irregular activities; (ii) the time, quantity and method of securities trading that are restricted by laws, regulations and the Exchange's business rules; (iii) abnormal trading activities that may affect the price or volume of securities trading; (iv) situations where the price or volume of securities trading is obviously abnormal; and (v) other matters that the China Securities Regulatory Commission or the Exchange deems necessary to focus on monitoring.
6.2 Abnormal trading behaviors that may affect securities trading prices or trading volumes include: (i) false orders, i.e., orders submitted and withdrawn in large quantities without the purpose of execution, in order to induce, mislead or influence the normal trading decisions of other investors; (ii) price manipulation, i.e. large orders, continuous orders, intensive orders, or orders submitted at prices significantly deviating from the latest transaction price of securities, during which the securities trading price rises or falls significantly; (iii) maintaining the price limit, i.e. maintaining the securities trading price at the price limit through large orders, continuous orders, and intensive orders; (iv) large or frequent self-buying and selling, mutual counterparty transactions or reverse transactions between individual accounts, accounts actually controlled by oneself, or suspected related accounts; (v) large orders, continuous orders, intensive orders, and intensive orders. (vi) Engaging in programmed trading by automatically generating or issuing trading instructions through computer programs, thereby affecting the security of the exchange's system or the normal trading order; (vii) Trading prices that deviate significantly from reasonable values, suspected of involving the transfer of benefits through securities trading; (viii) Influencing the trading price, settlement price, or reference value of related securities or their derivatives through abnormal reporting during the period when securities prices are sensitive; (ix) Engaging in a large number of continuous transactions of a single security over a period of time; (x) Using trading in other related markets to influence securities trading, or using securities market trading to influence trading in other related markets; (xi) Other abnormal trading behaviors that the China Securities Regulatory Commission or the exchange deems necessary to monitor closely. The exchange will conduct consolidated monitoring of single or multiple ordinary securities accounts, margin securities accounts, and other securities accounts (groups) suspected of being related, opened in the name of the investor or actually controlled by the same investor.
6.3 Situations where securities trading prices or trading volumes are significantly abnormal include: (i) securities business departments in the same securities business department or in the same region buying or selling the same security in large quantities; (ii) securities trading prices rising or falling significantly and deviating significantly from the rise or fall of the relevant index during the same period, and the listed company has no major announcements; (iii) other abnormal trading situations that the China Securities Regulatory Commission or the Exchange deems necessary to monitor closely.
6.4 As needed by the market, this exchange may, in conjunction with other securities and futures exchanges and other institutions, conduct investigations into situations such as those described in items 8 and 10 of Article 6.2.
6.5 Members shall monitor the securities trading activities of their clients in real time. If any abnormalities are found in a client's trading activity, the member shall promptly inform, remind, or warn the client. For abnormal trading activities that may seriously affect the order of securities trading or are suspected of being illegal or irregular, the member shall refuse to accept the client's commission in accordance with the securities trading agency agreement and promptly report to the exchange.
6.6 The Exchange may conduct on-site or off-site investigations into key matters under its supervision in securities trading, and members, their securities business departments, other trading participants, and investors shall cooperate.
6.7 During on-site or off-site investigations, the Exchange may, as needed, request relevant members and their securities business departments, other trading participants, and investors to provide the following documents and materials in a timely, accurate, and complete manner: (i) the investor's account opening information, power of attorney, fund account information, and trading information of relevant securities accounts; (ii) the actual controller and operator of relevant securities accounts or fund accounts, the source of funds, and an explanation of whether there is any connection between relevant accounts; (iii) an explanation of key monitoring matters in securities trading; and (iv) other materials related to key monitoring matters of the Exchange.
6.8 For serious violations among the key monitoring items listed in Article 6.1, the Exchange may take the following self-regulatory measures as appropriate: (i) verbal warning; (ii) written warning; (iii) meeting for discussion; (iv) requiring a written commitment; (v) including the securities account in the key monitoring accounts; (vi) suspending trading during the trading day; (vii) restricting trading after the market closes; (viii) other self-regulatory measures stipulated by the Exchange. If there is any objection to the measures in item (vii) above, an application for review may be submitted to the Exchange within 15 trading days from the date of receipt of the relevant decision or the date of public announcement of the relevant decision (whichever comes first). The relevant measures will not be suspended during the review period. The Exchange will promptly report any suspected insider trading, market manipulation, or trading using undisclosed information discovered during trading monitoring to the China Securities Regulatory Commission for investigation and prosecution.
6.9 The Exchange will monitor securities trading for risks. In the event of significant abnormal fluctuations, the Exchange may take measures such as restricting trading or forcibly suspending trading, and will report to the China Securities Regulatory Commission (CSRC); if the fluctuations seriously affect the stability of the securities market, the Exchange may take measures such as temporary market closure and issue a public announcement. Specific measures will be stipulated separately by the Exchange.
Chapter Seven: Handling Abnormal Transaction Situations
7.1 In order to maintain the normal order of securities trading and market fairness, the Exchange may decide to take measures such as technical suspension or temporary market closure if some or all of the following unforeseen events prevent the normal conduct of trading: (i) force majeure; (ii) unforeseen events; (iii) major technical failures; (iv) major human errors; (v) other abnormal circumstances as determined by the Exchange. If the unforeseen events stipulated in the preceding paragraph cause a major abnormality in the securities trading results, and settlement based on the trading results would have a significant impact on the normal order of securities trading and market fairness, the Exchange may take measures such as canceling the trading or notifying the securities registration and settlement institution to suspend settlement.
7.2 In the event of an inability to submit orders or an interruption in market data transmission, members should promptly report to the Exchange. If the number of securities branches experiencing inability to submit orders or interruption in market data transmission exceeds 10% of the total number of all member securities branches of the Exchange, it constitutes an abnormal trading situation, and the Exchange may implement a temporary market closure.
7.3 If the Exchange believes that an abnormal trading situation as stipulated in Articles 7.1 and 7.2 may occur and seriously affect the normal conduct of trading, it may decide to suspend trading technically or temporarily suspend the market. Upon the request of the China Securities Regulatory Commission, the Exchange implemented a temporary market suspension.
7.4 The Exchange will announce its decisions regarding technical suspensions, temporary market closures, cancellations of transactions, and notifications to securities registration and settlement institutions to postpone settlement, and will promptly report to the China Securities Regulatory Commission (CSRC). Once the reasons for a technical suspension or temporary market closure are resolved, the Exchange may decide to resume trading and will announce such decisions.
7.5 The Exchange shall not be liable for civil damages for losses caused by abnormal trading conditions, significant abnormal fluctuations, or the corresponding measures taken by the Exchange, except where there is gross negligence.
7.6 Specific regulations for handling abnormal trading situations will be formulated separately by the Exchange.
Chapter Eight Transaction Disputes
8.1 In the event of a transaction dispute between trading participants or between a member and a client, the relevant member and other trading participants shall record the relevant information for the Exchange's review. If a transaction dispute affects normal trading, the member and other trading participants shall promptly report it to the Exchange.
8.2 In the event of a transaction dispute between trading participants or between members and clients, the Exchange may provide necessary transaction data in accordance with relevant regulations.
8.3 Members are obligated to coordinate and resolve any questions a customer may have about a transaction.
Chapter Nine Transaction Costs
9.1 Investors who complete transactions of securities trading shall pay commissions to members in accordance with regulations.
9.2 Trading participants shall pay transaction handling fees and other fees to the Exchange as required; members shall also pay membership management fees to the Exchange as required.
9.3 The fee items, fee standards and management methods for securities transactions shall be implemented in accordance with relevant regulations.
Chapter Ten Supplementary Provisions
10.1 If a trading participant violates these rules, the Exchange will take self-regulatory measures or disciplinary actions in accordance with the relevant provisions of the "Shenzhen Stock Exchange Member Management Rules".
10.2 For securities issuance, subscription, purchase, redemption, exercise and other related transactions conducted through the Exchange's trading system, the relevant provisions of these rules shall apply; if the China Securities Regulatory Commission or the Exchange has other provisions, those provisions shall prevail.
10.3 The time mentioned in these rules shall be based on the time of the exchange's trading system.
10.4 The following terms in these Rules shall have the following meanings: (i) Market: refers to the securities trading market established by this Exchange. (ii) Order: refers to the act of an investor authorizing a member to buy or sell securities. (iii) Order: refers to the act of a trading participant sending a securities buy or sell instruction to the Exchange's trading system. (iv) Centralized Order Book: refers to all unexecuted order queues arranged by the trading system at a given point in time according to buy/sell direction and price priority and time priority. (v) Best Price in the Counterparty (Our Side) Queue: refers to the highest price for buyers or the lowest price for sellers in the centralized order book. (vi) Call Auction Reference Price: refers to the virtual call auction transaction price formed by all orders in the centralized order book according to the call auction rules as of the time of disclosure. (vii) Matched Quantity: refers to the virtual transaction quantity formed by all orders in the centralized order book according to the call auction rules as of the time of disclosure. (viii) Unmatched Quantity: refers to the remaining quantity of buyer or seller orders in the centralized order book at the call auction reference price that cannot be virtually executed at the call auction reference price as of the time of disclosure. (ix) Securities price sensitive period: refers to the specific period during which the transaction price, settlement price and reference value of relevant securities and their derivatives are calculated, including the specific time for calculating the conversion price of convertible bonds, the specific time for calculating the issuance price of securities, the specific time for calculating the net asset value per unit of securities, and the specific time for calculating the settlement price of derivatives, etc.
10.5 The meanings of terms not defined in these rules shall be determined in accordance with laws, administrative regulations, departmental rules, normative documents and the relevant business rules of this firm.
10.6 The terms “exceeding,” “below,” and “insufficient” in these rules do not include the number itself, while “reaching,” “above,” and “below” include the number itself.
10.7 This rule shall come into effect upon approval by the Board of Directors of this Exchange and the China Securities Regulatory Commission. The same applies to any amendments.
10.8 This rule is subject to interpretation by this firm.
10.9 This rule shall come into effect on the first day of listing of the first main board stock issued in accordance with the "Administrative Measures for the Registration of Initial Public Offerings of Stocks". Appendix 1
Shenzhen Stock Exchange Rules for Trading, Subscription and Redemption of Securities Investment Funds (Revised in 2022)

Chapter 1 General Provisions
Article 1. These Detailed Rules are formulated in accordance with the Securities Investment Fund Law, the Measures for the Operation and Management of Publicly Offered Securities Investment Funds, the Measures for the Sale of Securities Investment Funds, and the Trading Rules of the Shenzhen Stock Exchange (hereinafter referred to as the Exchange) to regulate the trading, subscription, purchase, and redemption (hereinafter referred to as "subscription and redemption") of fund units (hereinafter referred to as "funds") on the Shenzhen Stock Exchange (hereinafter referred to as the Exchange) and to protect the legitimate rights and interests of investors.
Article 2. These Rules apply to the trading, subscription, and redemption of fund units on this Exchange. Where these Rules do not provide otherwise, the Trading Rules and other relevant business rules of this Exchange shall apply.
Article 3 The trading, subscription, and redemption of fund units in venues other than this exchange shall be governed by the fund contract, fund prospectus, and the relevant regulations of the venue.
Article 4 The trading, subscription and redemption of fund units on this exchange do not represent the exchange's judgment or guarantee of the fund's investment value, and investors shall bear the investment risks of the fund themselves.
Article 5 When fund units are traded, subscribed, or redeemed on this exchange, the fund manager shall fulfill its information disclosure obligations in accordance with the law and fully disclose the risks of the fund products. Members of this exchange shall fully understand and assess clients' level of understanding of fund products and their risk tolerance, establish a sound client management and service system, and earnestly fulfill their responsibilities for investor suitability management.
Chapter Two: Trading

Section 1 General Provisions
Article 6. Closed-end funds, exchange-traded funds (ETFs), listed open-end funds (LOFs), structured funds, and other fund types recognized by the Exchange may be listed and traded on the Exchange. A closed-end fund refers to a fund listed and traded on the Exchange whose total number of fund units remains fixed during the term of the fund contract. An ETF refers to an open-end fund listed and traded on the Exchange, whose fund units are subscribed and redeemed using a combination of securities, cash, or other consideration stipulated in the fund contract, in a "unit subscription, unit redemption" manner. An LOF refers to an open-end fund listed and traded on the Exchange, whose fund units are subscribed and redeemed using cash, in a "amount subscription, unit redemption" manner. A structured fund refers to a fund that divides its fund units into sub-units with different expected risk and return according to the risk and return allocation method stipulated in the fund contract, wherein all or part of the sub-units are listed and traded or subscribed and redeemed on the Exchange. According to the fund contract, the basic units and sub-units of a structured fund can be paired and converted through splitting and merging. The specific rules for splitting and merging shall be separately stipulated by the Exchange or other handling venues.
Article 7 Fund unit transactions may be conducted through methods such as competitive bidding and block trading.
Article 8 The minimum price increment for fund unit transactions is RMB 0.001.
Article 9 On the first day of listing of fund units, the previous closing price displayed in the real-time market is the net asset value or reference net asset value (rounded to RMB 0.001) of the fund units most recently announced and provided to this institution by the fund manager, and the accuracy of the value is the responsibility of the fund manager.
Article 10. When a fund distributes its rights and interests, the ex-rights and ex-dividend process shall be carried out on the next trading day following the record date.
Article 11. If a tiered fund undergoes a share conversion, the closing price displayed in the real-time market on the next trading day after the conversion will be adjusted to the fund's net asset value per share or reference net asset value (rounded to RMB 0.001) most recently published by the fund manager and provided to this institution. The accuracy of this value is the responsibility of the fund manager.
Section 2 Competitive Bidding
Article 12. Fund unit auction transactions shall be conducted using either limit orders or market orders. The opening call auction period is from 9:15 to 9:25 on each trading day, the continuous auction period is from 9:30 to 11:30 and from 13:00 to 14:57, and the closing call auction period is from 14:57 to 15:00.
Article 13. When purchasing fund units through competitive bidding, the order quantity shall be 100 units or an integral multiple thereof. When selling fund units, any remaining balance less than 100 units shall be sold in a single order. The maximum order quantity for a single competitive bidding transaction of fund units shall not exceed 1 million units.
Article 14. Price fluctuation limits shall be imposed on fund unit auction transactions, with a fluctuation limit of 10%.
Section 3 Bulk Transactions
Article 15. If the number of fund units in a single transaction is not less than 2 million units, or the transaction amount is not less than RMB 2 million, block trading may be used.
Article 16. For fund units traded via negotiated block trades, the Exchange accepts orders from 9:15 to 11:30 and 13:00 to 15:30 on each trading day, and confirms the transaction from 15:00 to 15:30 on each trading day. For fund units traded via after-hours pricing block trades, the Exchange accepts orders from 15:05 to 15:30 on each trading day.
Article 17 The price range for block trades of fund unit agreements shall be 10% above and below the previous closing price.
Chapter Three: Subscription, Purchase, and Redemption

Section 1 General Provisions
Article 18. ETFs, LOFs, listed open-ended funds, and other fund types recognized by the Exchange may be subscribed to and redeemed through the Exchange. Closed-end funds may be subscribed to through the Exchange. Listed open-ended funds refer to open-ended funds that are subscribed to and redeemed through the Exchange but are not listed for trading.
Article 19 When a fund manager conducts fund unit subscription and redemption business through this exchange, it shall submit an application to this exchange and sign an agreement with this exchange.
Article 20 The application period for fund unit subscription and redemption is from 9:15 to 11:30 and from 13:00 to 15:00 on each trading day.
Article 21 Members of this Exchange who act as agents for investors in the subscription and redemption of fund units shall possess fund sales business qualifications and be recognized by this Exchange and its designated clearing and settlement institution (hereinafter referred to as the "clearing and settlement institution"). Members of this Exchange participating in the subscription and redemption of ETF units shall also reach an agreement with the relevant fund manager and sign a formal agreement.
Article 22 When accepting subscription and redemption orders from investors, members of this exchange shall verify that investors have sufficient cash, portfolio securities, other consideration, or fund units.
Article 23 The Exchange may suspend acceptance of fund unit subscription and redemption applications under any of the following circumstances: (i) abnormal circumstances prevent subscription and redemption from proceeding normally; (ii) the ETF manager fails to publish the ETF subscription and redemption information list (hereinafter referred to as the "subscription and redemption list") before the market opens, or the content of the subscription and redemption list contains material errors; (iii) the fund manager applies to suspend subscription and redemption in accordance with the provisions of the fund contract and fund prospectus; (iv) the clearing and settlement institution requests the Exchange to suspend the subscription and redemption of money market ETFs; (v) other circumstances deemed appropriate by the Exchange. The fund manager shall issue a public announcement when the Exchange suspends acceptance of fund unit subscription and redemption applications. The Exchange shall resume accepting subscription and redemption applications when the aforementioned circumstances are eliminated, and the fund manager shall issue a public announcement.
Article 24. In the event of serious consequences caused by force majeure, accidents, technical failures, illegal intrusion, or other reasons, the Exchange may take appropriate measures or declare the subscription, purchase, or redemption invalid.
Section Two Subscription
Article 25. Subscriptions of fund units shall be submitted in multiples of 1,000 units. The number of fund units subscribed shall also comply with the provisions of the fund contract and the fund prospectus.
Article 26 Investors may subscribe to fund units repeatedly during the fundraising period, and once the subscription application is submitted, it cannot be withdrawn.
Article 27 Investors shall use cash to subscribe for fund units on this exchange.
Section 3: ETF Share Subscription and Redemption
Article 28. The creation and redemption of ETF units shall be submitted in accordance with the minimum creation and redemption unit or an integer multiple thereof as stipulated in the fund contract and fund prospectus.
Article 29 The application instructions for the creation and redemption of ETF units shall include the securities account, securities code, buying and selling direction, quantity, etc. Once the application instructions are confirmed, they shall not be changed or revoked.
Article 30. Fund managers shall provide the Exchange with an ETF subscription and redemption list before the market opens each day. The format and content of the subscription and redemption list shall comply with the relevant regulations of the Exchange, and fund managers shall ensure the truthfulness, accuracy, and completeness of the content of the subscription and redemption list. The Exchange shall process subscription and redemption applications that meet the requirements in accordance with the provisions of the fund contract and the fund prospectus, and shall treat subscription and redemption applications that do not meet the requirements as invalid applications.
Article 31. Exchange members acting on behalf of investors in ETF share creation and redemption shall provide a comprehensive overview of the relevant business rules to investors making their first ETF share creation or redemption, fully disclose the potential risks, and require them to sign a risk disclosure statement, except for money market ETFs. The format of the risk disclosure statement shall be determined by the fund manager.
Section 4 Subscription and Redemption of LOF Shares
Article 32. Subscriptions of LOF units are submitted in monetary terms, with a unit of RMB 1; redemptions are submitted in units, with a unit of 1 fund unit. The number of LOF unit subscriptions and redemptions shall comply with the relevant provisions of the fund contract and fund prospectus.
Article 33. Redemption applications for LOF units may be withdrawn before 15:00 on the same day.
Article 34 The fund manager or its entrusted registration and settlement institution is responsible for confirming the subscription and redemption results of LOF units.
Article 35 The subscription and redemption of open-ended fund units listed for trading are subject to the provisions of this section.
Regulation.
Chapter Four: Rules for the Use of Shares
Article 36. Investors who purchase bond ETF units, gold ETF units, listed money market fund units, cross-border ETF units, cross-border LOF units, and commodity futures ETF units through competitive bidding (hereinafter referred to as "competitive bidding purchase") on the same day may sell them on the same day. Investors who purchase bond ETF units, gold ETF units, listed money market fund units, cross-border ETF units, cross-border LOF units, and commodity futures ETF units through block trading (hereinafter referred to as "block trading") on the same day may sell them through block trading (hereinafter referred to as "block selling") on the next trading day, and may sell them through competitive bidding (hereinafter referred to as "competitive bidding sale") on the following trading day. The cross-border ETF units and cross-border LOF units mentioned in paragraphs 1 and 2 of this article are limited to open-ended fund units whose tracked index constituent securities or investment targets implement same-day turnaround trading. Other fund units purchased by investors on the same day may be sold on the following trading day.
Article 37 When investors trade, subscribe to, or redeem single-market stock ETFs, they shall comply with the following provisions: (i) ETF units purchased through competitive bidding on the same day may be redeemed on the same day; ETF units purchased through block trading on the same day may be redeemed on the next trading day; (ii) ETF units subscribed to on the same day may be sold through competitive bidding on the same day, and may be redeemed or sold through block trading on the next trading day; (iii) Stocks obtained from redemption on the same day may be sold through competitive bidding on the same day, and may be used to subscribe to ETF units or sold through block trading on the next trading day; (iv) Stocks purchased through competitive bidding on the same day may be used to subscribe to ETF units on the same day; Stocks purchased through block trading on the same day may be used to subscribe to ETF units on the next trading day.
Article 38 When investors trade or redeem single-market bond ETFs with a portfolio of securities as consideration, they shall comply with the following provisions: (i) ETF units purchased through competitive bidding on the same day may be redeemed on the same day; ETF units purchased through block trading on the same day may be redeemed on the next trading day; (ii) ETF units subscribed on the same day may be redeemed or sold through competitive bidding on the same day, and may be sold through block trading on the next trading day; (iii) Bonds redeemed on the same day may be used to subscribe for ETF units, sold through competitive bidding, or declared as collateral on the same day, and may be sold through block trading on the next trading day; (iv) Bonds purchased through competitive bidding or released from pledge on the same day may be used to subscribe for ETF units on the same day; bonds purchased through block trading on the same day may be used to subscribe for ETF units on the next trading day. When investors trade or redeem cross-market bond ETFs whose constituent securities of the tracked index include interbank bond market trading instruments, they shall comply with the following provisions: (i) ETF units purchased on the same day may be redeemed on the next trading day; (ii) ETF units subscribed on the same day may not be sold or redeemed before settlement.
Article 39 When investors trade or redeem single-market bond ETFs, cross-market bond ETFs, gold ETFs, commodity futures ETFs or cross-border ETFs with cash consideration, they shall comply with the following provisions: (i) ETF units purchased through competitive bidding on the same day may be redeemed on the same day; ETF units purchased in bulk on the same day may be redeemed on the next trading day; (ii) ETF units subscribed on the same day may not be sold or redeemed before settlement.
Article 40 When investors trade or subscribe to/reduce cross-market stock ETFs, they shall comply with the following provisions: (i) ETF units purchased through competitive bidding on the same day may be redeemed on the same day; ETF units purchased through block trading on the same day may be redeemed on the next trading day; (ii) ETF units subscribed through the Exchange on the same day may be sold through competitive bidding on the same day, and may be redeemed or sold through block trading on the next trading day; (iii) Stocks redeemed through the Exchange on the same day may be sold through competitive bidding on the same day, and may be used to subscribe to ETF units or sold through block trading on the next trading day; (iv) Stocks purchased through competitive bidding on the same day may be used to subscribe to ETF units on the same day; Stocks purchased through block trading on the same day may be used to subscribe to ETF units on the next trading day; (v) ETF units subscribed through the registration and settlement institution on the same day may not be sold or redeemed before settlement.
Article 41 When investors trade or redeem gold ETFs using spot contracts on the Shanghai Gold Exchange as consideration, they shall comply with the following provisions: (1) ETF units purchased on the same day can be redeemed on the next trading day; (2) ETF units subscribed on the same day can be redeemed or sold through competitive bidding on the same day, and can be sold in bulk on the next trading day. Redemption of gold ETFs using spot contracts on the Shanghai Gold Exchange as consideration shall be processed through the Shanghai Gold Exchange.
Article 42 When investors trade or subscribe to/redeem money market ETFs, they shall comply with the following provisions: (1) ETF units purchased through competitive bidding on the same day may be redeemed on the same day; ETF units purchased through block trading on the same day may be redeemed on the next trading day; (2) ETF units subscribed on the same day may be redeemed or sold through competitive bidding on the same day, and may be sold through block trading on the next trading day.
Article 43 When investors trade or subscribe to LOF, they shall comply with the following provisions: (1) LOF units purchased on the same day may be redeemed on the next trading day; (2) LOF units subscribed on the same day may not be sold or redeemed before settlement.
Article 44. Investors may not redeem open-ended fund units purchased on the same day before settlement.
Article 45. The Exchange may adjust the above-mentioned share usage rules as needed by the market. The share usage rules that investors must comply with when trading, subscribing to, or redeeming other fund shares will be separately stipulated by the Exchange.
Chapter Five Information Release
Article 46 The fund manager shall calculate the net asset value per unit of the fund in accordance with the relevant provisions of the fund contract and the fund prospectus, and may publish it through the market information release system of the exchange on trading days. The fund manager shall be responsible for the accuracy of the calculation and the handling of any errors.
Article 47 The fund manager shall publish the ETF creation and redemption list through the information release channels designated by the Exchange every trading day. The creation and redemption list shall not be modified after publication.
Article 48. Fund managers may publish the reference net asset value (NAV) per ETF unit in real time through the exchange's market data release system during trading hours for investors' reference. The calculation methods for the reference NAV per ETF unit include: (i) the fund manager providing the exchange with the calculation method for the reference NAV per ETF unit, and the exchange calculating the reference NAV per ETF unit; (ii) the fund manager calculating the reference NAV per ETF unit and providing it to the exchange; and (iii) the fund manager entrusting a third party to calculate the reference NAV per ETF unit and providing it to the exchange. If a fund manager entrusts a third party to calculate the reference NAV per ETF unit, an agreement shall be signed with the third party.
Chapter Six Supervision and Management
Article 49 If a member violates these rules, the firm shall take self-regulatory or disciplinary measures against him in accordance with the Membership Management Rules and other relevant regulations.
Article 50 If a fund manager violates these Rules, the Exchange may order it to make corrections and take the following self-regulatory or disciplinary measures depending on the severity of the circumstances: (i) verbal warning; (ii) written warning; (iii) meeting and talking; (iv) public criticism; (v) public censure; (vi) collection of punitive liquidated damages; (vii) reporting to the China Securities Regulatory Commission; (viii) other self-regulatory measures and disciplinary measures stipulated by the Exchange.
Article 51 If a member violates the relevant regulations of the registration and settlement institution, the Exchange may restrict its participation in relevant fund business upon the request of the registration and settlement institution.
Chapter Seven Supplementary Provisions
Article 52 The registration, custody and settlement of fund unit transactions, subscriptions and redemptions shall be handled by the registration and settlement institution in accordance with relevant rules.
Article 53 The fee standards and methods for fund unit transactions shall be implemented in accordance with the relevant regulations of this Exchange; the fee standards and methods for fund unit subscription and redemption shall be separately stipulated by this Exchange.
Article 54. The following terms in these Rules shall have the following meanings: (i) Single-market equity ETF: refers to an ETF whose constituent securities of the tracked stock index are only stocks listed on the Shanghai Stock Exchange. (ii) Single-market bond ETF: refers to an ETF whose constituent securities of the tracked bond index are only bonds listed on the Shanghai Stock Exchange. (iii) Cross-market equity ETF: refers to an ETF whose constituent securities of the tracked stock index include, in addition to stocks listed on the Shanghai Stock Exchange, stocks traded in other domestic markets or stocks eligible for the Hong Kong Stock Connect, and whose subscription and redemption consideration includes the portfolio securities. (iv) Cross-market bond ETF: refers to an ETF whose constituent securities of the tracked bond index include bonds not listed on the Shanghai Stock Exchange. (v) Cross-border ETF: refers to an ETF whose constituent securities of the tracked index or investment targets include overseas securities, and whose subscription and redemption are based on cash. (vi) Gold ETF: refers to an ETF that invests in spot contracts of the Shanghai Gold Exchange and tracks the spot contract price. (vii) Money market ETF: refers to an ETF that invests only in money market instruments. (viii) Commodity Futures ETF: refers to an ETF that primarily uses the strategy of holding commodity futures contracts listed and traded on commodity futures exchanges established with the approval of the China Securities Regulatory Commission, aims to track commodity futures prices or price indices, and uses a combination of commodity futures contracts or the method stipulated in the fund contract for subscription and redemption.
Article 55 This set of detailed rules shall be interpreted by this firm.
Article 56 These Detailed Rules shall come into effect on the date of promulgation. The "Detailed Rules for the Implementation of Trading, Subscription and Redemption of Securities Investment Funds of Shenzhen Stock Exchange (Revised in December 2020)" (Shenzhen Stock Exchange [2020] No. 730) issued on December 4, 2020, shall be repealed simultaneously.

Shenzhen Stock Exchange Pilot Program for Listing and Trading of Stocks or Depositary Receipts of Innovative Enterprises (Shenzhen Stock Exchange Document No. 284 of 2018)
Chapter 1 General Provisions
Article 1 This regulation is formulated to standardize the listing and trading of shares and depositary receipts of pilot innovative enterprises (hereinafter referred to as "innovative enterprises") on the Shenzhen Stock Exchange (hereinafter referred to as "the Exchange"), protect the legitimate rights and interests of investors, and maintain the order of the securities market, in accordance with the Securities Law of the People's Republic of China, the Several Opinions on Conducting Pilot Programs for Domestic Issuance of Shares or Depositary Receipts by Innovative Enterprises (hereinafter referred to as the "Several Opinions"), the Administrative Measures for Issuance and Trading of Depositary Receipts (Trial) (hereinafter referred to as the "Depositary Receipt Management Measures"), the Implementation Measures for Supervision of Domestic Issuance and Listing of Shares or Depositary Receipts by Pilot Innovative Enterprises, the Implementation Measures for Continuous Supervision of Domestic Issuance of Shares or Depositary Receipts by Innovative Enterprises (Trial) (hereinafter referred to as the "Continuous Supervision Implementation Measures"), and other laws, administrative regulations, departmental rules, normative documents (hereinafter collectively referred to as "domestic laws"), as well as the Shenzhen Stock Exchange Stock Listing Rules, the Shenzhen Stock Exchange Growth Enterprise Market Stock Listing Rules (hereinafter collectively referred to as the "Listing Rules"), and the Shenzhen Stock Exchange Trading Rules (hereinafter referred to as the "Trading Rules").
Article 2 This Regulation applies to the listing and information disclosure of publicly issued shares of innovative enterprises and publicly issued depositary receipts of red-chip companies not yet listed overseas on this Exchange; where this Regulation does not provide for such matters, the Listing Rules and other relevant regulations of this Exchange shall apply. This Regulation also applies to the listing and information disclosure of publicly issued depositary receipts of red-chip companies already listed overseas on this Exchange. This Regulation applies to the trading of publicly issued shares or depositary receipts of innovative enterprises after their listing on this Exchange; where this Regulation does not provide for such matters, the Trading Rules and other relevant provisions of this Exchange's business rules regarding stock trading shall apply. For the purposes of this Regulation, "innovative enterprise" refers to a company that, in accordance with the "Several Opinions," has been approved by the China Securities Regulatory Commission to publicly issue shares or depositary receipts, including innovative enterprises registered domestically (hereinafter referred to as domestic innovative companies) and innovative enterprises registered overseas but whose main business activities are located in China (hereinafter referred to as red-chip companies).
Article 3. Innovative enterprises and their directors, supervisors, senior management, shareholders or holders of depositary receipts, domestic representatives for information disclosure, actual controllers, acquirers, depositaries, sponsors and their representatives, securities service institutions and their related personnel, etc., shall comply with domestic laws, the Listing Rules, the Trading Rules, these Measures, and other relevant regulations of the Exchange, and accept the self-regulatory supervision of the Exchange. For the purposes of these Measures, "directors, supervisors, and senior management" refers to the directors, supervisors, senior management personnel, or personnel exercising similar powers of innovative enterprises. Where there are no supervisors, a board of supervisors, or personnel or organizations exercising similar powers, the provisions of these Measures and other relevant regulations of the Exchange concerning supervisors and board of supervisors shall not apply.
Article 4. In the event of specific operational risks for innovative enterprises, or for red-chip companies with differential voting rights, variable interest structures, or similar special arrangements, the relevant circumstances, particularly information on risks and corporate governance, shall be fully and thoroughly disclosed, as well as all measures to protect the legitimate rights and interests of investors in accordance with the law. The depositary shall faithfully and diligently perform its duties and obligations in accordance with domestic laws, the exchange's business rules, other relevant regulations, and the depositary agreement, and shall not infringe upon the legitimate rights and interests of the holders of depositary receipts.
Article 5 When innovative enterprises list their shares or depositary receipts on this exchange, they shall sign a listing agreement with this exchange, which shall specify the rights and obligations of both parties and other relevant matters.
Article 6. For innovative enterprises whose stocks or depositary receipts are listed and traded on this exchange, they shall be registered, deposited and settled by China Securities Depository and Clearing Corporation Limited (hereinafter referred to as China Securities Depository and Clearing).
Chapter Two: Listing

Section 1 Initial Public Offering and Listing of Domestic Innovative Companies
Article 7 Domestic innovative companies applying for the listing of their initial public offering (IPO) shares on this exchange shall meet the listing conditions for IPO shares as stipulated in the Listing Rules.
Article 8. Domestic innovative companies applying for the listing of their initial public offering shares on this exchange shall submit listing application documents to this exchange in accordance with the provisions of the Listing Rules.
Article 9. Domestic innovative companies shall disclose a listing announcement and other documents in accordance with the Listing Rules within five trading days prior to their stock listing. The listing announcement shall comply with the content and format requirements stipulated by the Exchange and shall prominently disclose the material matters and major risks specifically highlighted in the prospectus. The company's controlling shareholder, actual controller, and directors, supervisors, and senior management shall make relevant commitments in accordance with domestic laws and the Listing Rules and disclose them in the listing announcement.
Article 10. The delisting of shares of domestic innovative companies shall be governed by domestic laws and the provisions of the Listing Rules, unless otherwise stipulated by the Exchange. When determining whether a company's shares have triggered delisting risk warnings, suspension of listing, or termination of listing in accordance with the Listing Rules and these Measures, the relevant fiscal year shall be calculated from the first full fiscal year after the company's shares are listed.
Article 11 Share reductions by domestic innovative companies after listing shall comply with domestic laws, the Listing Rules, and the Implementation Rules for Share Reduction by Shareholders, Directors, Supervisors, and Senior Management of Listed Companies on the Shenzhen Stock Exchange (hereinafter referred to as the "Implementation Rules for Share Reduction"), and other relevant business rules. If a domestic innovative company is not yet profitable at the time of listing, its controlling shareholder, actual controller, directors, and senior management shall not reduce their shareholdings held before the company becomes profitable.
Section 2. Initial Public Offering or Listing of Red Chip Companies' Shares or Depositary Receipts in China
Article 12 A red-chip company applying for the listing of its domestically issued shares on the exchange shall meet the listing conditions for initial public offerings of shares as stipulated in the Listing Rules.
Article 13 A red-chip company applying for the listing of its initial public offering (IPO) of depositary receipts on this exchange shall meet the following conditions: (i) the number of depositary receipts issued in this IPO shall not be less than 100 million, or the market value of the depositary receipts issued in this IPO at the time of listing shall not be less than RMB 5 billion; (ii) the company has not committed any major illegal acts in the past three years, and its financial accounting reports contain no false records; (iii) other conditions required by this exchange. A red-chip company with differential voting rights arrangements applying for the listing of its IPO of shares and depositary receipts on this exchange shall also meet the provisions of Section 3 of this chapter.
Article 14 When a red-chip company applies to the Exchange for the listing of its initial public offering (IPO) shares in China, it shall submit listing application documents in accordance with the Listing Rules and other relevant regulations (excluding documents concerning share custody, appointment of a board secretary, and the lock-up of shares prior to the IPO). It shall also submit to the Exchange proof that the shares issued in this domestic offering have been deposited with China Securities Depository and Clearing Corporation Limited (CSDC), information on the company's securities affairs office established in China, and its appointed domestic representative for information disclosure, and other relevant materials. If, according to the company law and other laws and regulations of the overseas place of registration and the company's articles of association or related documents (hereinafter referred to as the Articles of Association), the red-chip company is not required to submit a shareholders' meeting resolution for the domestic IPO, it may not submit such a resolution when applying for listing, but it shall submit a relevant board resolution.
Article 15. A red-chip company applying to the Exchange for the listing of its initial public offering (IPO) of depositary receipts in China shall submit the following documents: (i) a listing application form; (ii) the approval document from the China Securities Regulatory Commission (CSRC) for the IPO; (iii) the board resolution and shareholders' meeting resolution (if applicable) for the listing of the depositary receipts; (iv) a copy of the business license or company registration document; (v) the company's articles of association; (vi) the issuer's financial reports and related financial information for the most recent three years, audited by an accounting firm qualified to conduct securities and futures related business in accordance with Chinese auditing standards; (vii) the signed depositary agreement and custody agreement; (viii) the custody certificate issued by the custodian for the underlying securities represented by the depositary receipts; and (ix) [Further details to be added]. (x) Sponsorship agreement and listing sponsorship letter issued by the sponsor; (xi) Legal opinion issued by the law firm; (xi) Capital verification report issued by the accounting firm with securities and futures related business qualifications after the completion of this issuance; (xii) Proof that the depositary receipts issued by the Company in this issuance have been deposited with China Securities Depository and Clearing Corporation Limited; (xiii) Declaration and commitment letter of the directors (supervisors, senior management personnel); (xiv) Relevant information of the securities affairs agency established by the Company in China and the domestic representative for information disclosure; (xv) Financial data and explanation of relevant major matters newly added in accordance with regulations after this issuance and before listing (if applicable); (xvi) The most recent prospectus and the complete set of issuance application materials reviewed by the China Securities Regulatory Commission; (xvii) Listing announcement; (xviii) Other documents required by the Exchange.
Article 16. Red-chip companies shall, within five trading days prior to the listing of their shares or depositary receipts, disclose relevant documents such as the listing announcement, listing sponsorship letter, and legal opinion in accordance with the regulations of this Exchange. The listing announcement shall include the following: (i) an overview of the offering and listing arrangements; (ii) the main differences and impacts between the company's governance practices and the corporate governance rules followed by domestic listed companies; (iii) information on risks and corporate governance arising from the company's differential voting rights, contractual control structure, or similar special arrangements, as well as various measures to protect the legitimate rights and interests of investors in accordance with the law; (iv) any significant changes to the matters disclosed in the company's prospectus prior to the listing of the company's shares or depositary receipts; (v) commitments made by the company and its controlling shareholder, actual controller, and directors, supervisors, and senior management (if applicable); and (vi) other matters required to be disclosed by this Exchange. If a red-chip company has already disclosed the matters stipulated in the preceding paragraph in its prospectus, it may disclose them in the listing announcement by reference.
Article 17 The listing announcement submitted by a red-chip company to the Exchange may be signed by a senior manager authorized by the company. The red-chip company shall ensure that the contents of the listing application documents submitted to the Exchange are true, accurate and complete, and that there are no false records, misleading statements or material omissions.
Article 18. Listing application documents and ongoing information disclosure documents submitted by red-chip companies shall be in Chinese. Red-chip companies and related information disclosure obligors shall disclose listing and ongoing information disclosure documents in accordance with the regulations of the China Securities Regulatory Commission (CSRC) and the Exchange, on the information disclosure media designated by the CSRC, the official website of the Exchange, and the CNINFO website.
Article 19. Red-chip companies shall establish a securities affairs office in China and appoint a domestic information disclosure representative to handle information disclosure and regulatory liaison matters during the listing period of the company's shares or depositary receipts. The domestic information disclosure representative shall possess the corresponding qualifications of a board secretary of a domestic listed company, be familiar with domestic information disclosure regulations and requirements, and be proficient in Chinese. Red-chip companies shall establish effective communication channels with domestic investors, regulatory agencies, and the exchange, safeguard the legitimate rights and interests of domestic investors in accordance with regulations, and maintain smooth communication with domestic regulatory agencies and the exchange.
Article 20 When a red-chip company applies to the Exchange for the listing of its initial public offering of shares or depositary receipts in China, it shall engage domestic sponsors, law firms, accounting firms qualified to conduct securities and futures related business, and other securities service institutions to provide relevant services. Sponsors and their representatives shall comply with the relevant regulations of the Exchange regarding listing sponsorship. Domestic sponsors and securities service institutions may engage overseas institutions to assist in their work, but this shall not exempt them from their legally mandated responsibilities.
Article 21 The delisting of shares of red-chip companies listed on this Exchange shall be governed by domestic laws, the Listing Rules, and Article 10 of these Measures, unless otherwise stipulated by the Exchange. The delisting of depositary receipts of red-chip companies listed on this Exchange shall, in principle, be governed by the Listing Rules and Article 10 of these Measures, except for delisting indicators involving the proportion of public shareholding and cumulative trading volume within a certain period. Specific matters shall be separately stipulated by the Exchange. Upon termination of the listing of depositary receipts, the red-chip company and the depositary shall fulfill their relevant obligations in accordance with the provisions of the Depositary Receipts Management Measures and the depositary agreement to protect the legitimate rights and interests of depositary receipt holders.
Article 22 When a red-chip company issues depositary receipts and lists on the exchange, the actual controller shall undertake not to voluntarily relinquish his or her status as the actual controller within three years of the domestic listing. When a red-chip company that is not yet profitable issues shares and lists on the exchange, or when a red-chip company that is not yet profitable and not yet listed overseas issues depositary receipts and lists on the exchange, its controlling shareholder, actual controller, directors, and senior management shall undertake not to reduce their holdings of shares held before the listing until the company achieves profitability.
Article 23. When shareholders or depositary receipt holders of red-chip companies reduce their holdings of domestic shares or depositary receipts, they shall comply with domestic laws, the Listing Rules, the Implementation Rules for Share Reduction, and these Measures. When directors, supervisors, senior management personnel, and domestic representatives of red-chip companies reduce their holdings of domestically issued shares or depositary receipts of the company, they shall comply with the relevant provisions of the Implementation Measures for Continuous Supervision and the Implementation Rules for Share Reduction regarding share reduction by directors, supervisors, and senior management personnel.
Article 24 When a red-chip company issues or privately placed shares or depositary receipts to strategic investors and other investors with restricted sale arrangements within China, it shall submit listing and trading application documents to the Exchange in accordance with the Listing Rules and other business regulations of the Exchange after the expiration of the lock-up period for the relevant shares or depositary receipts. After the Exchange approves the listing and trading application for the shares or depositary receipts, the company shall disclose a preliminary announcement within three trading days prior to the listing and trading of the relevant shares or depositary receipts. The preliminary announcement shall include the following: (i) the listing and trading time and quantity of the restricted shares or depositary receipts; (ii) the lock-up commitments made by the relevant shareholders or holders and their fulfillment; and (iii) other content required by the Exchange.
Section 3 Special Requirements for Red Chip Companies Listing with Differential Voting Rights Arrangements
Article 25 Shareholders holding special voting rights shares shall be persons who have made significant contributions to the development or business growth of the red-chip company and who have continuously served as directors of the company or in other positions approved by the board of directors before and after the listing of the red-chip company, or shareholding entities actually controlled by such persons. The total shares held by special voting rights shareholders in the red-chip company shall reach more than 10% of the total issued voting shares of the company.
Article 26 The articles of association of a red-chip company shall stipulate the voting rights of each special voting rights share.
Quantity. The number of voting rights for each ordinary voting share of a red chip company shall not be zero, and the number of voting rights for each special voting share shall not exceed 20 times the number of voting rights for each ordinary voting share.
Article 27 After the listing of shares and depositary receipts of a red-chip company on this exchange, except for pro rata share placements, capital increases through share transfers, and situations stipulated in the company's articles of association prior to the domestic public offering, the company shall not issue special voting rights shares domestically or overseas, nor shall it increase the proportion of special voting rights. The special voting rights proportion referred to in these Measures means the ratio of the number of voting rights of all special voting rights shares to the total number of voting rights of all issued shares of the red-chip company.
Article 28. A red-chip company shall ensure that the proportion of ordinary voting rights is not less than 10% and shall have the right to propose to convene an extraordinary general meeting of shareholders; the shares for which a shareholder proposes a resolution for the general meeting of shareholders shall not exceed 10% of the total issued voting shares of the company. The proportion of ordinary voting rights referred to in these Measures means the proportion of the number of voting rights of all ordinary voting shares to the number of voting rights of all issued shares of the company.
Article 29. In any of the following circumstances, special voting rights shares shall be converted into ordinary voting rights shares at a ratio of 1:1: (i) The shareholder holding special voting rights shares no longer meets the qualifications stipulated in Article 25, Paragraph 1 of these Measures, or loses the corresponding ability to perform duties, or dies; (ii) The shareholder holding special voting rights shares transfers the special voting rights shares held to another person, or entrusts the voting rights of the special voting rights shares to another person, except for the transfer or entrustment to an entity that meets the qualifications stipulated in Article 25, Paragraph 1 of these Measures or an entity actually controlled by the special voting rights shareholder; (iii) A change of control occurs in the red-chip company. In the event of the circumstance (iii) above, all special voting rights shares issued by the red-chip company shall be converted into ordinary voting rights shares.
Article 30 When a shareholder of a red-chip company exercises voting rights on the following matters, the number of voting rights of special voting shares shall be the same as that of ordinary voting shares: (i) making substantial amendments to the company's articles of association; (ii) changing the shareholder rights included in special voting shares or ordinary voting shares; (iii) merger, division, dissolution, or change of company form. A red-chip company may stipulate in its articles of association that any change in the number of voting rights of each special voting share or ordinary voting share requires approval by a certain proportion of the number of voting rights held by holders of that type of share.
Article 31. Differences in voting rights arrangements between red-chip companies already listed overseas and the provisions of this section exist.
In cases of discrepancies, the provisions of the laws and regulations of the overseas place of registration, the rules of the overseas listing location, and the company's articles of association shall apply. The company shall provide a detailed explanation of the differences and their causes, as well as the corresponding measures to protect the legitimate rights and interests of investors in accordance with the law.
Article 32 Shareholders holding special voting rights shall exercise their rights in accordance with applicable laws and the company's articles of association, and shall not abuse their special voting rights or use them to harm the legitimate rights and interests of domestic investors. If any of the circumstances described in the preceding paragraph occur and harm the legitimate rights and interests of domestic investors, this Exchange may require the red-chip company and the shareholders holding special voting rights to rectify the situation.
Chapter Three: Transactions
Article 33 Members shall formulate relevant working systems for investor suitability management of innovative enterprise stocks or depositary receipts, fully understand the client situation of those participating in innovative enterprise stock or depositary receipt business, and provide clear suitability matching opinions. Members shall, through appropriate means, fully inform investors of various risks associated with innovative enterprise stocks and depositary receipts, remind investors to pay attention to investment risks, and guide them to participate in online issuance, subscription, and trading rationally and in a standardized manner.
Article 34 Members shall require investors to sign a relevant risk disclosure statement in writing or electronically before participating in the online subscription or trading of shares or depositary receipts of innovative enterprises for the first time. Members shall not accept subscription or purchase orders from clients who have not signed the risk disclosure statement.
Article 35 Investors shall fully understand and be aware of the nature of the innovative enterprise's shares and depositary receipts, the differences in rights between domestic and foreign shareholders and depositary receipt holders, the specific operational risks of the innovative enterprise, differential voting rights arrangements, variable interest entity (VIE) structures or similar special arrangements, and other risk matters, as well as domestic laws and the exchange's business rules. Investors shall, in light of their own risk awareness and tolerance, prudently determine whether to participate in the transaction. Holding depositary receipts constitutes becoming a party to the depositary agreement, and is deemed to have agreed to and abide by the terms of the depositary agreement.
Article 36 The Exchange may, in an appropriate manner, mark the depositary receipts of innovative enterprises listed on the Exchange, as well as the shares or depositary receipts of innovative enterprises that are not yet profitable or have differential voting rights arrangements listed on the Exchange. Specific matters shall be separately stipulated by the Exchange.
Article 37 The pricing currency for red-chip company depositary receipts is RMB, and the pricing unit is "price per depositary receipt." The minimum price increment is RMB 0.01. When purchasing red-chip company depositary receipts through competitive bidding, the order quantity must be 100 receipts or an integral multiple thereof, and the maximum order quantity in a single transaction shall not exceed 1 million receipts. Any remaining balance less than 100 receipts must be sold in a single order. The Exchange may adjust the pricing unit, order quantity, and minimum price increment for red-chip company depositary receipts as needed by the market and will announce such adjustments to the market.
Article 38. The trading of depositary receipts of red-chip companies is subject to price fluctuation limits, with a daily price fluctuation limit of 10%. No price fluctuation limits apply on the first day of listing of depositary receipts; specific trading arrangements shall be in accordance with the relevant regulations of the Exchange regarding the first day of listing of initial public offerings (IPOs). If a red-chip company undergoes equity distribution, capitalization of reserves, rights issue, or other similar events, the Exchange, upon the company's application, shall, in accordance with the relevant provisions of the Trading Rules regarding ex-rights and ex-dividend, process the ex-rights and ex-dividend treatment of the company's depositary receipts listed on the Exchange, unless otherwise stipulated by the Exchange.
Article 39 For transactions involving no less than 300,000 depositary receipts of a red-chip company, or a transaction amount of no less than RMB 2 million, block trades may be used. The negotiated transfer of red-chip company depositary receipts shall be handled in accordance with the relevant business rules of the Exchange for negotiated transfer of shares.
Article 40 The auction trading of depositary receipts of red-chip companies shall be included in the real-time market data of the Exchange.
Article 41. If any of the following circumstances occur with the depositary receipts of a red-chip company, the Exchange shall publish the names of the five member securities business departments or trading units with the largest buy and sell amounts on that day, along with their respective buy and sell amounts: (i) The daily closing price deviation reaches ±7% and ranks among the top five; (ii) The daily price fluctuation reaches 15% and ranks among the top five; (iii) The daily turnover rate reaches 20% and ranks among the top five. The corresponding classification index for calculating the price deviation in item (i) of the preceding paragraph is the Shenzhen A-share Index compiled by the Exchange.
Article 42. Any of the following circumstances in the competitive bidding trading of depositary receipts of red-chip companies shall constitute abnormal fluctuations, and the Exchange shall publish the names of the five member securities business departments or trading units with the largest cumulative purchase and sale amounts during the period of abnormal fluctuations, along with their respective cumulative purchase and sale amounts: (i) The cumulative deviation of the closing price within three consecutive trading days reaches ±20%; (ii) The cumulative deviation of the daily closing price within three consecutive trading days for ST and *D depositary receipts reaches ±12%; (iii) The ratio of the average daily turnover rate within three consecutive trading days to the average daily turnover rate of the previous five trading days reaches 30 times, and the cumulative turnover rate of the security within three consecutive trading days reaches 20%; (iv) Other circumstances deemed abnormal fluctuations by the China Securities Regulatory Commission or the Exchange. The corresponding classification index for calculating the deviation of price fluctuations in item (i) of the preceding paragraph is the Shenzhen A-share Index compiled by the Exchange. The abnormal fluctuation index shall be recalculated from the date the relevant information disclosure obligor issues an abnormal fluctuation announcement or resumes trading.
Article 43 Members shall, in accordance with the requirements of the Trading Rules, the Shenzhen Stock Exchange Member Management Rules (hereinafter referred to as the "Member Management Rules") and other business rules of the Exchange, earnestly fulfill their responsibilities for managing client trading behavior and promptly detect, stop and report any abnormal trading behavior of clients in the trading of innovative enterprise stocks and depositary receipts.
Article 44 During the listing period of a red-chip company's depositary receipts on this exchange, they shall not be converted into underlying securities overseas, unless otherwise stipulated by the China Securities Regulatory Commission or this exchange.
Chapter Four Continuous Information Disclosure

Section 1 General Provisions
Article 45. Innovative enterprises and related information disclosure obligors shall promptly disclose all material events that may have a significant impact on the trading price of their shares, depositary receipts, and their derivatives. Innovative enterprises and related information disclosure obligors shall ensure that the continuous information disclosure documents submitted to the Exchange are true, accurate, and complete, and that there are no false records, misleading statements, or material omissions. If innovative enterprises and related information disclosure obligors submit documents or disclose matters to shareholders, actual controllers, market institutions, or other third parties involving undisclosed material information, they shall require the relevant parties to strictly fulfill their confidentiality obligations. If material information has already been leaked, it shall be disclosed promptly.
Article 46. Innovative enterprises and related information disclosure obligors shall disclose material information fairly to all investors and shall not disclose or leak it to individual or partial investors in advance. When innovative enterprises communicate with any institution or individual regarding the company's production and operation status, financial status, etc., through performance briefings, analyst meetings, roadshows, investor surveys, or other occasions, they shall not provide any material information that has not yet been disclosed by the company.
Article 47 Innovative enterprises shall prudently disclose new technologies, new business formats, new models, and new products that have not yet achieved commercial application, and shall not exaggerate, generalize, or mislead investors. If innovative enterprises voluntarily disclose predictive information or other information with significant uncertainty, they shall be cautious and objective, and shall not use such information to improperly affect the trading prices of the company's stocks, depositary receipts, and their derivatives.
Article 48. Innovative enterprises shall closely monitor major media reports or market rumors concerning the company. If such reports or rumors have a significant impact on the trading price of the company's shares, depositary receipts, and their derivatives, the company shall promptly verify them and disclose or clarify them as necessary. If the Exchange believes that such reports or rumors may have a significant impact on the trading price of the company's shares, depositary receipts, and their derivatives, it may require the company to verify or clarify them.
Article 49. If the trading of shares, depositary receipts, and their derivatives of an innovative enterprise reaches the abnormal fluctuation standard stipulated in the Exchange's business rules, the company shall promptly verify whether there is any information that should be disclosed but has not been disclosed, and disclose an abnormal fluctuation announcement. Even if the trading of shares, depositary receipts, and their derivatives of an innovative enterprise does not reach the abnormal fluctuation standard, but the trading experiences significant fluctuations or sustained abnormalities, the company shall promptly verify whether there is any information that should be disclosed but has not been disclosed. If the relevant circumstances may have a significant impact on the trading price of the company's shares, depositary receipts, and their derivatives, the company shall disclose this information promptly. When an innovative enterprise verifies the relevant circumstances in accordance with the preceding two paragraphs, it may apply to the Exchange for a trading suspension. If the company does not apply for a trading suspension, the Exchange may, as appropriate, require the company to suspend trading for verification and disclose the relevant circumstances.
Article 50. Innovative enterprises shall establish and strictly implement an information disclosure management system. Innovative enterprises and related information disclosure obligors shall disclose relevant information through the direct access method in accordance with the provisions of the "Shenzhen Stock Exchange Guidelines for Listed Companies' Information Disclosure Express Service". Information disclosure and other filing documents submitted by innovative enterprises to the Exchange shall be submitted through the relevant business areas of the Exchange. Specific business procedures shall be handled in accordance with the relevant provisions of the Exchange's "Guidelines for Listed Companies' Business Handling No. 1 – Information Disclosure Business Handling" and "Guidelines for Listed Companies' Business Handling No. 2 – Obtaining Digital Certificates".
Article 51 If the information to be disclosed by an innovative enterprise is uncertain, constitutes a temporary trade secret, or falls under other circumstances recognized by the Exchange, and timely disclosure may harm the company's interests or mislead investors, disclosure may be postponed. If the information to be disclosed by an innovative enterprise constitutes a state secret or trade secret, and disclosure or fulfillment of relevant obligations in accordance with the Listing Rules and these Measures may lead to violations of relevant national laws and regulations on confidentiality, unfair competition, or harm to the company's interests, disclosure may be waived. For an innovative enterprise to postpone or waive disclosure of relevant information pursuant to the preceding two paragraphs, the following conditions must be met: (i) the relevant information has not been leaked; (ii) relevant insiders have made a written commitment to confidentiality; and (iii) the trading of the shares or depositary receipts has not experienced abnormal fluctuations. Innovative enterprises shall prudently determine the scope of matters for which disclosure is postponed or waived and shall not arbitrarily expand it. If the reasons for postponement or waiver of disclosure have been eliminated or no longer meet the conditions stipulated in the preceding paragraph, the company shall disclose this information promptly.
Article 52. If the application of the relevant information disclosure requirements and ongoing supervision provisions of this Exchange to red-chip companies and related information disclosure obligors may lead to difficulties in complying with the relevant regulations of their place of registration or overseas listing location, they may apply to this Exchange for adjustment of the application, but shall explain the reasons and alternative solutions, and engage a law firm to issue a legal opinion. If this Exchange believes that the application should not be adjusted according to law, the red-chip company and related parties shall comply with the relevant provisions of this Exchange.
Article 53 Shareholders, actual controllers, directors, supervisors, and senior management of red-chip companies, as well as investors holding depositary receipts issued by the company domestically and overseas, shall, in accordance with the relevant provisions of the "Implementation Measures for Continuous Supervision" and the business rules of this Exchange, promptly fulfill their information disclosure obligations regarding changes in equity, acquisitions, and changes in holdings of the company's domestic shares or depositary receipts. The equity held by investors and their concerted parties directly or indirectly in shares or depositary receipts issued by red-chip companies domestically and overseas shall be calculated aggregated. If the change in the depositary's holdings of the underlying overseas securities of a red-chip company due to the depositary arrangement reaches the standard for changes in the equity of a listed company, the provisions of this Exchange concerning the disclosure of information on changes in the equity of listed companies shall not apply.
Article 54. Directors, supervisors, senior management, and domestic representatives of red-chip companies shall not trade the company's shares or depositary receipts issued in China during the following periods: (i) within 30 days before the announcement of periodic reports; if the announcement date of annual or semi-annual reports is postponed due to special reasons, the period shall be calculated from 30 days before the originally scheduled announcement date to the day before the announcement; (ii) within 10 days before the announcement of earnings forecasts or preliminary earnings reports (if applicable); (iii) from the date of occurrence of a major event that may have a significant impact on the trading price of the company's shares, depositary receipts, and their derivatives, to two trading days after the legally required disclosure; (iv) other periods stipulated by the China Securities Regulatory Commission and the Exchange.
Article 55. The controlling shareholder and actual controller of a red-chip company shall not trade the company’s shares or depositary receipts issued in China during the following periods: (i) within 30 days before the announcement of the annual report; if the announcement date of the annual report is postponed due to special reasons, the period shall be calculated from 30 days before the originally scheduled announcement date until the day before the announcement; (ii) within 10 days before the announcement of the company’s performance forecast or preliminary performance report (if applicable); (iii) from the date of occurrence of a major event that may have a significant impact on the trading price of the company’s shares, depositary receipts and their derivatives, until two trading days after the legally required disclosure; (iv) other periods stipulated by the China Securities Regulatory Commission and the Exchange.
Section 2 Continuous Information Disclosure by Domestic Innovation Companies
Article 56. Domestic innovative companies shall, in their annual reports, disclose the following business and technical information in light of the policy environment of their respective industries, such as the Internet, big data, cloud computing, artificial intelligence, software and integrated circuits, high-end equipment manufacturing, or biomedicine, as well as the development status of new technologies, new industries, new business formats, and new models: (1) The basic characteristics of the industry in which they operate, the main technical thresholds and barriers to entry, and the development status and future trends of relevant new technologies, new industries, new business formats, and new models during the reporting period. (2) Analysis of the main business models, operating models, profit models, and related uniqueness, as well as the innovative position and leading role in the industry; analysis of the competitiveness of the core management team and technical team; R&D investment during the reporting period, and core technology reserves for which relevant rights certificates or approval documents have been obtained. (3) If the company has not yet made a profit or has accumulated losses, it shall analyze the causes of such situations in light of the characteristics of the industry and fully disclose the impact of not yet making a profit or having accumulated losses on the company's cash flow, business expansion, talent attraction, team stability, R&D investment, strategic investment, and sustainability of production and operation. (4) Other operating information that may have a significant impact on the trading price of the company's stock and its derivatives. If there are significant changes to the disclosure items stipulated in the preceding paragraph that may have a substantial impact on the trading price of the company's shares and their derivatives, the company and the relevant information disclosure obligors shall disclose such changes in a timely manner.
Article 57. Domestic innovative companies shall, in their annual reports, disclose potential operational risks arising from significant changes in technology, products, policies, or business models, taking into account the characteristics of innovative businesses, such as high investment, rapid iteration, high risk, and vulnerability to disruption. They shall also fully disclose the risk of declining performance or losses. If a company is not yet profitable or has accumulated losses, it shall disclose the relevant circumstances in a prominent position in its annual report, particularly the risk of future inability to generate profits or distribute profits, and disclose all measures implemented in accordance with the law to protect the legitimate rights and interests of investors.
Article 58. If a domestic innovative company undertakes a major acquisition, asset disposal, or other transaction or arrangement that may result in a significant change in the company's business composition, it shall promptly disclose the following matters and fulfill the corresponding review procedures: (i) the basic information of the original business composition; (ii) the specific reasons for and rationality of the change in business composition, and the possible impact on the company; (iii) the basic information, feasibility analysis, and risk warnings of the new business; (iv) the technology, R&D progress, and commercialization status on which the new business depends; (v) an explanation of the approvals already obtained or pending from relevant departments for the new business (if applicable); (vi) the opinions of independent directors, the supervisory board, and the sponsor on the significant changes in the company's business composition; and (vii) other important information that the exchange or the company deems necessary to disclose.
Section 3 Continuous Information Disclosure for Red Chip Companies Not Yet Listed Overseas

Article 59. Red-chip companies that are not yet listed overseas shall, in accordance with the provisions of Section 2 of this Chapter, submit an annual report...
The report discloses information on innovative businesses and technologies, specific operational risks, and fulfills its obligation to disclose temporary information in a timely manner in light of significant changes in relevant matters.
Article 60. Red-chip companies not yet listed overseas that have differential voting rights, contractual control structures, or similar special arrangements shall disclose in their annual reports the implementation and changes of such arrangements during the reporting period, as well as the implementation of measures to protect the legitimate rights and interests of domestic investors under such arrangements. If there are significant changes or adjustments to the matters stipulated in the preceding paragraph that may have a substantial impact on the trading price of the company's shares, depositary receipts, and their derivatives, the company and relevant information disclosure obligors shall disclose this information promptly.
Article 61. If a red-chip company that has not yet listed overseas lists its depositary receipts on this exchange, it shall disclose in its annual and interim reports the implementation and changes of the depositary and custody arrangements during the reporting period, as well as the list and holdings of the top ten domestic depositary receipt holders at the end of the reporting period. The company shall promptly fulfill its temporary information disclosure obligations in any of the following circumstances: (i) changes in the depositary or custodian; (ii) pledge, misappropriation, judicial freezing, or other changes in ownership of the underlying assets of the depositary; (iii) significant amendments to the depositary agreement or custody agreement; (iv) changes in the conversion ratio between the depositary receipts and the underlying securities; (v) other circumstances deemed necessary to disclose by the China Securities Regulatory Commission and this exchange. After the listing of depositary receipts, the red-chip company shall not change the conversion ratio between the depositary receipts and the underlying securities without the consent of this exchange. In the event of the circumstances stipulated in paragraphs (i) and (ii) of this article, or in the event of significant amendments to the custody agreement, the depositary shall promptly inform the red-chip company, and the company shall promptly disclose such information.
Article 62. Red-chip companies not yet listed overseas and depositary institutions shall reasonably arrange the time and manner in which holders of depositary receipts exercise their rights, ensuring they have sufficient time and convenient conditions to exercise their corresponding rights, and shall promptly disclose the time, manner, specific requirements, and results of the exercise of rights by holders of depositary receipts in accordance with the depositary agreement. When red-chip companies and depositary institutions solicit voting intentions from holders of depositary receipts through the Shenzhen Stock Exchange's online voting system for shareholders' meetings, the specific business procedures shall be handled in accordance with the "Implementation Rules for Online Voting at Shareholders' Meetings of Listed Companies on the Shenzhen Stock Exchange," and the company and depositary institution shall announce this to the market in accordance with the depositary agreement.
Article 63. Red-chip companies not yet listed overseas shall fulfill the following temporary information disclosure obligations: (i) If there are significant changes to the relevant matters disclosed in the listing application documents, the company shall disclose them in a timely manner; (ii) If the company intends to issue shares or depositary receipts overseas, the company shall disclose the basic information of the issuance in a timely manner; (iii) If there are changes in the company's place of registration or domestic laws, the company shall disclose the significant impact on the matters disclosed in the listing application documents and periodic reports in a timely manner; (iv) If the company's securities are intended to be listed and traded on an overseas market, the company shall disclose in a timely manner the differences between the regulatory system of the overseas listing location and the regulatory system of the exchange, and the potential adverse impact on the rights and interests of domestic investors; (v) Information that should be disclosed in accordance with the company law and other laws and regulations of the overseas place of registration and the rules of the overseas listing location shall be disclosed simultaneously. In addition to the provisions of the preceding paragraph, the company shall also promptly disclose other information that has a significant impact on the trading price of the company's domestic shares, depositary receipts and their derivatives or on investors' investment judgments, as well as information necessary for full and fair disclosure.
Article 64. Red-chip companies that have not yet conducted business in accordance with Chapters 9 and 10 of the Listing Rules shall be subject to the provisions of these Rules.
For major transactions, related-party transactions, and other matters requiring shareholder approval as stipulated in the company's articles of association, the authority and procedures stipulated in the company's overseas place of registration and other relevant laws and regulations, as well as the company's articles of association, may be carried out in accordance with the provisions of domestic laws or the China Securities Regulatory Commission, unless otherwise stipulated by domestic laws or the China Securities Regulatory Commission. The company shall promptly disclose any relevant matters submitted to the shareholders' meeting for deliberation in accordance with the preceding paragraph.
Article 65 If there are different provisions or arrangements regarding the duties of the board of directors and independent directors in the company law or other laws, regulations or practices of the overseas place of registration of red-chip companies listed overseas, resulting in the board of directors and independent directors being unable to perform their duties or express their opinions in accordance with the provisions of this firm, the red-chip company shall explain the situation and reasons in detail and engage a law firm to issue legal opinions on the above matters.
Section 4 Continuous Information Disclosure of Red Chip Companies Listed Overseas
Article 66. For shares or depositary receipts issued domestically by red-chip companies already listed overseas and listed on this exchange, their ongoing information disclosure and regulatory matters shall be governed by Articles 60 to 62 of Sections 1, 2, and 3 of this chapter, as well as the provisions of this section.
Article 67. Red-chip companies listed overseas and related information disclosure obligors shall ensure that information disclosed in overseas markets is simultaneously disclosed in the domestic market, ensuring that domestic investors can obtain the same information equally, and shall not disclose or leak undisclosed material information to individual or partial investors, whether domestic or overseas. When a company discloses information in an overseas market that does not fall within the information disclosure period of this exchange, it shall disclose it within the most recent information disclosure period of this exchange.
Article 68. Information disclosure documents submitted by red-chip companies listed overseas and related information disclosure obligors in the domestic market shall be consistent with the content of their information disclosure documents submitted in their overseas listing locations, except for those prepared in accordance with different accounting standards and disclosed in accordance with domestic laws and relevant regulations of the China Securities Regulatory Commission. If there are substantial differences in the content of information disclosure documents between the domestic and overseas markets, the company and related information disclosure obligors shall provide a special explanation to the Exchange and disclose a correction or supplementary announcement as required by the Exchange.
Article 69. Red-chip companies that are already listed overseas shall prepare and disclose annual reports, interim reports and quarterly reports in accordance with the provisions of the "Implementation Measures for Continuous Supervision".
Article 70. Red-chip companies already listed overseas may decide whether to disclose earnings forecasts, preliminary earnings reports, and profit projections in accordance with the rules of the overseas listing location. If a company discloses the aforementioned information in its overseas listing location, it shall simultaneously disclose it in the domestic market. When disclosing earnings forecasts, preliminary earnings reports, and profit projections, companies shall be cautious and objective, and shall not use exaggerated, vague, or misleading statements, nor shall they use such information to improperly influence the trading prices of their shares, depositary receipts, and their derivatives.
Article 71. Red-chip companies listed overseas shall promptly disclose the following transactions that meet the disclosure standards stipulated in Article 72 of these Measures: (i) significant acquisitions or asset disposals (excluding asset purchases or sales related to the company's daily operations and not constituting asset swaps); (ii) overseas investments; (iii) entering into technology purchase or usage agreements; (iv) transferring or acquiring major R&D projects; (v) leasing in or leasing out assets; (vi) signing management contracts (including entrusted operation, entrusted management, etc.); (vii) donating or receiving assets; and (viii) other significant transactions stipulated by the China Securities Regulatory Commission and the Exchange.
Article 72. A red-chip company listed overseas shall promptly fulfill its temporary information disclosure obligations if it engages in any of the transactions stipulated in Article 71 of these Measures and meets one of the following criteria: (i) The total assets involved in the transaction (where both book value and appraised value exist, the higher value shall prevail) account for more than 10% of the company's total assets in the most recent audited financial statements; (ii) The transaction amount (including liabilities and expenses assumed) accounts for more than 10% of the company's net assets in the most recent fiscal year; (iii) The operating revenue related to the transaction target (such as equity) in the most recent fiscal year accounts for more than 10% of the company's audited operating revenue in the most recent fiscal year, and the amount exceeds RMB 50 million. Even if the relevant transaction does not meet the criteria stipulated in the preceding paragraph, but may have a significant impact on the trading price of the company's shares, depositary receipts, and their derivatives, it shall also be disclosed promptly.
Article 73. Red-chip companies listed overseas shall promptly fulfill their disclosure obligations regarding material transactions, related-party transactions, and other material matters at the earliest of the following points: (i) when the board of directors or the supervisory board makes a resolution on the material event; (ii) when the relevant parties sign a letter of intent or agreement on the material event; (iii) when directors, supervisors, and senior management become aware of the occurrence of the material event and report it. If the material event stipulated in the preceding paragraph involves significant uncertainty, and immediate disclosure may harm the company's interests or mislead investors, and the relevant insiders have made a written commitment to confidentiality, the company shall disclose it in the domestic market no later than when the relevant parties make a final resolution on the material event, sign a final agreement document, or, although no agreement has been signed, the transaction is certain to be completed. If relevant information has been leaked or market rumors have emerged, causing significant fluctuations in the trading prices of the company's shares, depositary receipts, and their derivatives, the company shall disclose this immediately.
Article 74. A red-chip company listed overseas shall promptly fulfill its temporary information disclosure obligations if it engages in any of the following related-party transactions with related parties: (i) transactions with related natural persons exceeding RMB 10 million; (ii) transactions with related legal persons exceeding RMB 50 million, and accounting for more than 0.1% of the listed company's most recent audited total assets; (iii) other circumstances that the exchange or the company deems may lead to a bias in interests between the company and its related parties. The identification of related parties and related relationships of a red-chip company listed overseas shall be carried out in accordance with the disclosure standards used when a red-chip company first applies for a domestic public offering of shares or depositary receipts.
Article 75. For related-party transactions of red-chip companies already listed overseas, as stipulated in Article 74 of these Measures, if the pricing meets one of the following fair market conditions, the transaction may be disclosed only in the annual report and interim report: (i) priced according to government-set prices or reasonably priced within the range of government-guided prices; (ii) priced according to open market prices; (iii) priced according to methods such as public bidding or public auction. If the related-party transaction does not meet the fair market conditions stipulated in the preceding paragraph, the company shall disclose it promptly.
Article 76. If any of the following material events occur to a red-chip company listed overseas, and based on the materiality of the relevant events, it is determined that they may have a significant impact on the trading price of the company's shares, depositary receipts, and their derivatives, the company shall disclose them in a timely manner, explaining the cause of the event, its current status, and the possible legal consequences: (i) providing guarantees or financial assistance to external parties; (ii) major litigation or arbitration matters involving an amount exceeding 10% of the absolute value of the company's most recent audited total assets; (iii) receiving substantial government subsidies or other additional income; (iv) other material matters stipulated by the China Securities Regulatory Commission and the Exchange. If the Exchange believes that the relevant matters may have a significant impact on the trading price of the company's shares, depositary receipts, and their derivatives, it may require the company to disclose the relevant information in a timely manner. In addition to the disclosure obligations stipulated in paragraph 1 of this Article, the company shall, in its annual report, classify and summarize the disclosure of material external guarantees and financial assistance to related parties that occur during the reporting period and meet the requirements of the Listing Rules for submission to the shareholders' meeting for review.
Article 77. For red-chip companies already listed overseas that engage in material transactions or related-party transactions as defined in this section...
For other material matters, the company may proceed in accordance with the powers and procedures stipulated in the laws and regulations of its overseas place of registration, the rules of its overseas listing location, and its articles of association, unless otherwise stipulated by domestic law or the China Securities Regulatory Commission. If the company submits relevant matters to the shareholders' meeting for deliberation in accordance with the preceding paragraph, it shall disclose this information in a timely manner.
Article 78. If the laws and regulations of the overseas place of registration of a red-chip company, the rules of the overseas listing location, or the standards generally accepted in practice have different provisions or arrangements regarding the duties of the company's board of directors and independent directors, resulting in the board of directors and independent directors being unable to perform their duties or express opinions in accordance with the relevant provisions of this firm, the red-chip company shall provide a detailed explanation of the circumstances and reasons and engage a law firm to issue a legal opinion on the above matters. If a red-chip company listed overseas and its directors, supervisors, and senior management have signed written confirmations, made declarations, or commitments in accordance with relevant domestic regulations, they may, without changing the substantive content, make appropriate adjustments to the wording of the confirmations, declarations, or commitments in accordance with the requirements of the laws and regulations of the overseas place of registration, the relevant rules of the overseas listing location, or the standards generally accepted in practice.
Article 79. If a red-chip company already listed overseas is required to suspend trading, be suspended from listing, or have its listing terminated in its overseas listing location, it shall promptly notify the Exchange and make a disclosure. The Exchange will handle the matter according to the circumstances. To ensure timely and fair information disclosure, the Exchange may, based on the company's application or actual circumstances, decide on the suspension and resumption of trading of the company's domestic shares or depositary receipts.
Chapter Five Self-Discipline Management
Article 80 When supervising the entities stipulated in Article 3 of these Measures, the Exchange may take the following measures: (i) requiring innovative enterprises and other regulated entities to provide explanations and clarifications on relevant issues and disclose them; (ii) requiring innovative enterprises to engage relevant securities service institutions to verify existing problems and issue opinions; (iii) issuing various notices and letters; (iv) meeting with relevant personnel; (v) requiring rectification; (vi) conducting on-site inspections of innovative enterprises; (vii) reporting relevant illegal and irregular activities to the China Securities Regulatory Commission; (viii) issuing regulatory recommendation letters to relevant competent authorities; (ix) other regulatory measures. The entities stipulated in the preceding paragraph shall accept and actively cooperate with the Exchange's daily supervision, truthfully answer the Exchange's inquiries within the prescribed time limit, submit explanations as required, and disclose corresponding correction or supplementary announcements as needed. Unless otherwise stipulated in these Measures, companies and related information disclosure obligors shall not fail to fulfill their information disclosure obligations on the grounds that relevant matters are uncertain or require confidentiality.
Article 81. If a domestic innovative company, a red-chip company not yet listed overseas, or any related information disclosure obligor violates these Measures, the Listing Rules, other relevant business rules of the Exchange, or any statements or commitments made by it, the Exchange may, depending on the severity of the circumstances, impose corresponding regulatory measures or disciplinary sanctions as stipulated in the Listing Rules and other business rules. If a depositary has any of the circumstances stipulated in the preceding paragraph, the Exchange may, depending on the severity of the circumstances, impose disciplinary sanctions such as public criticism or public reprimand.
Article 82. If a red-chip company listed overseas or its related information disclosure obligors violates these Measures or its declarations and commitments, the Exchange may, depending on the severity of the circumstances, impose one or more of the following disciplinary actions: (i) issue a notice of criticism or public censure to the company, the related information disclosure obligor, or the depositary; (ii) issue a notice of criticism or public censure to the company's directors, supervisors, and senior management, and publicly determine that they are unsuitable to serve as directors, supervisors, or senior management of a listed company; (iii) issue a notice of criticism or public censure to the company's domestic information disclosure representative, and publicly determine that they are unsuitable to serve as the domestic information disclosure representative of a red-chip company; (iv) issue a notice of criticism or public censure to the sponsor and its sponsor representatives, securities service institutions and their related personnel, bankruptcy administrators and related personnel, and temporarily suspend the acceptance of their issued documents. If the entities mentioned in the preceding paragraph violate these Measures or their declarations and commitments, and the circumstances are serious, the Exchange shall report the matter to the China Securities Regulatory Commission for investigation and handling in accordance with the law.
Article 83 The Exchange shall implement regulatory measures or disciplinary actions against red-chip companies listed overseas and related information disclosure obligors in accordance with the "Implementation Rules for Self-Regulatory Measures and Disciplinary Actions of the Shenzhen Stock Exchange" and other relevant regulations, and record such actions in the integrity file as required. Companies and related information disclosure obligors may apply to the Exchange for hearings and reviews in accordance with the scope and procedures stipulated in the "Detailed Rules for Hearing Procedures of the Shenzhen Stock Exchange Self-Regulatory Supervision" and the "Detailed Rules for the Working of the Appeals Review Committee of the Shenzhen Stock Exchange." Article 84 In accordance with the provisions of the Trading Rules, these Measures, and other relevant business rules of the Exchange, the Exchange shall take corresponding regulatory measures against abnormal trading behavior of investors in the trading of innovative enterprise stocks or depositary receipts; and shall report suspected insider trading, market manipulation, and other illegal and irregular activities to the China Securities Regulatory Commission for investigation and handling in accordance with the law. For abnormal trading behavior that seriously affects the order or fairness of securities trading, the Exchange may take measures such as restricting account trading of relevant investors and report to the China Securities Regulatory Commission.
Article 85 If a member fails to fulfill its responsibilities for managing the suitability of investors in innovative enterprises or managing the trading behavior of clients in accordance with these Measures and other relevant business rules of the Exchange, or violates other provisions of these Measures, the Exchange may, depending on the severity of the circumstances, impose corresponding regulatory measures or disciplinary sanctions as stipulated in the Membership Management Rules and other business rules.
Article 86. With the approval of the China Securities Regulatory Commission (CSRC), the Exchange may establish the following cross-market regulatory cooperation mechanisms with regulatory agencies and exchanges in the overseas listing locations of red-chip companies: (i) information exchange mechanism; (ii) risk warning and prevention mechanism; (iii) joint investigation mechanism; (iv) self-regulatory punishment assistance mechanism; and (v) other necessary cooperation mechanisms. For the purpose of conducting market supervision, the Exchange may, in accordance with the cross-market regulatory cooperation mechanisms stipulated in the preceding paragraph, provide or obtain relevant information on self-regulatory entities from relevant domestic and overseas regulatory agencies and exchanges.
Article 87 This firm may send documents to innovative enterprises and related overseas institutions or individuals by means of public announcement, delivery to the domestic representative of the red chip company for information disclosure, etc.
Chapter Six Supplementary Provisions
Article 88. The listing, trading and other fees for depositary receipts of red-chip companies shall be charged in accordance with the relevant standards for A-shares on the main board of this exchange.
Article 89. The following terms in these Measures shall have the following meanings: (i) Red-chip companies already listed overseas refer to red-chip companies that, at the time of their initial public offering and listing on the domestic market, have already publicly issued shares or depositary receipts and listed on the overseas market. Red-chip companies that simultaneously conduct public offerings or listings of shares or depositary receipts on the domestic and overseas markets and meet the market capitalization requirements for red-chip companies already listed overseas as stipulated in the "Several Opinions" after the overseas issuance are completed shall be deemed as red-chip companies already listed overseas. (ii) Differential voting rights refer to the issuance of different classes of shares by a company, in which the voting rights of one class of shares are superior to those of another class of shares. Among them, shares with superior voting rights are special voting rights shares, and vice versa are ordinary voting rights shares. (iii) VIE structure refers to an investment structure in which a red-chip company actually controls the operating entity in the domestic market through a contractual agreement. (iv) Achieving profitability refers to an innovative enterprise that was not profitable at the time of listing achieving profitability within a full fiscal year after listing.
Article 90 In these Measures, the terms “less than,” “lower than,” “insufficient,” and “exceeding” do not include the number itself, while “above” and “reaching” include the number itself.
Article 91. Unless otherwise specified, the term "yuan" in these Measures refers to Renminbi Yuan.
Article 92 The formulation and amendment of these Measures shall be approved by the Board of Directors of the Exchange and submitted to the China Securities Regulatory Commission for approval.

Special Rules for Trading on the ChiNext Board of the Shenzhen Stock Exchange
Chapter 1 General Provisions
1.1 These provisions are formulated to regulate trading activities on the ChiNext board of the Shenzhen Stock Exchange (hereinafter referred to as "the Exchange"), maintain the order of the securities market, and protect the legitimate rights and interests of investors, in accordance with the Securities Law of the People's Republic of China, the Administrative Measures for Securities Exchanges, and other laws and regulations, as well as the Trading Rules of the Shenzhen Stock Exchange (hereinafter referred to as "the Trading Rules") and other business rules.
1.2 This regulation applies to the trading of stocks and depositary receipts listed on the Growth Enterprise Market (GEM) of this Exchange, as well as related funds. Where this regulation does not provide for a specific matter, the Trading Rules and other relevant regulations of this Exchange shall apply.
Chapter Two: Competitive Bidding
2.1 The Exchange applies price fluctuation limits to ChiNext stocks in its competitive bidding transactions, with a daily price fluctuation limit of 20%. Orders exceeding the price fluctuation limit are invalid. For ChiNext stocks undergoing initial public offerings (IPOs), there are no price fluctuation limits for the first five trading days after listing. The Exchange may indicate this in an appropriate manner.
2.2 For trading ChiNext stocks, the valid price range for limit orders during continuous trading shall meet the following requirements: (i) the buy order price shall not exceed 102% of the buy benchmark price; (ii) the sell order price shall not be lower than 98% of the sell benchmark price. The buy (sell) benchmark price mentioned above refers to the lowest sell (highest buy) order price displayed immediately; if there is no lowest sell (highest buy) order price displayed immediately, it refers to the highest buy (lowest sell) order price displayed immediately; if there is no highest buy (lowest sell) order price displayed immediately, it refers to the most recent transaction price; if there are no transactions on the day, it refers to the previous closing price. The provisions of the preceding two paragraphs do not apply to limit orders placed during temporary trading halts during market hours.
2.3 The calculation result of the effective bidding range shall be rounded to the minimum price fluctuation unit according to the principle of rounding. If the absolute value of the difference between the upper or lower limit of the effective bidding range and the benchmark price is lower than the minimum price fluctuation unit, the effective bidding range shall be the benchmark price plus or minus one minimum price fluctuation unit.
2.4 Limit orders that exceed the valid bidding range cannot participate in the bidding immediately and are temporarily stored in the trading system; when price fluctuations bring them into the valid bidding range, the trading system will automatically retrieve the orders and allow them to participate in the bidding.
2.5 The following situations constitute abnormal intraday fluctuations in the trading of ChiNext stocks and will result in a temporary intraday suspension by the Exchange: (i) When the intraday trading price of a stock without price limits rises or falls by 30% or more from the opening price of the day; (ii) When the intraday trading price of a stock without price limits rises or falls by 60% or more from the opening price of the day; (iii) Other situations deemed by the China Securities Regulatory Commission or the Exchange to constitute abnormal intraday fluctuations.
2.6 For intraday temporary suspensions implemented in accordance with the preceding article, the following provisions shall apply: (i) The duration of a single intraday temporary suspension shall be 10 minutes; (ii) If the suspension period extends beyond 14:57, trading shall resume at 14:57 on the same day, and a resumption call auction shall be held for accepted orders, followed by a closing call auction; (iii) During the intraday temporary suspension period, orders may be submitted or withdrawn, and an intraday call auction shall be held for accepted orders upon resumption of trading. The specific time of intraday temporary suspensions shall be subject to the announcement of the Exchange.
2.7 The Exchange may make separate provisions regarding the effective bidding range and intraday temporary suspension of trading for stocks listed on the Growth Enterprise Market (GEM), and may make adjustments based on market conditions.
2.8 For stocks listed on the ChiNext board, the number of shares in a single limit order shall not exceed 300,000 shares, and the number of shares in a single market order shall not exceed 150,000 shares.
2.9 The Exchange applies a 20% daily price fluctuation limit to the following funds traded via auction: (i) Index ETFs, LOFs, or Class B shares of leveraged funds whose tracking index constituents consist solely of ChiNext stocks or other stocks subject to a 20% daily price fluctuation limit; (ii) LOFs whose fund contracts stipulate that at least 80% of their non-cash fund assets are invested in ChiNext stocks or other stocks subject to a 20% daily price fluctuation limit. The Exchange will publish a list of funds with a 20% daily price fluctuation limit to the market.
Chapter 3 After-Hours Pricing Trading
3.1 After-hours pricing trading refers to a trading method in which buy and sell orders placed after the close of trading on the ChiNext board are matched continuously, one by one, based on the principle of time priority and the closing price of the day.
3.2 The after-hours pricing trading period is from 15:05 to 15:30 on each trading day. The submission period for after-hours pricing trading is from 9:15 to 11:30 and from 13:00 to 15:30 on each trading day. If trading is suspended during the market opening period, submissions can continue during the suspension period. If trading is still suspended at 15:00 on the same day, after-hours pricing trading will not be conducted. Unexecuted submissions can be cancelled during the submission period. Cancellation instructions are valid only after confirmation by the exchange's trading system. After-hours pricing submissions are valid only for the day of submission.
3.3 Investors who buy or sell stocks through after-hours pricing transactions shall submit after-hours pricing orders to the member. After-hours pricing orders shall include the securities account number, securities code, buy/sell direction, limit price, and order quantity.
3.4 After-hours pricing orders from members and other trading participants shall include the securities account number, securities code, trading unit code, securities brokerage identification code, buy/sell direction, limit price, and quantity. After-hours pricing orders for Shenzhen-Hong Kong Stock Connect shall include the brokerage client code, securities code, brokerage code, buy/sell direction, limit price, and quantity.
3.5 After-hours pricing orders with a buy limit price lower than the closing price or a sell limit price higher than the closing price are invalid.
3.6 The number of shares in a single after-hours pricing order shall not exceed 1 million shares.
3.7 During the after-hours pricing trading period, the exchange uses the closing price as the transaction price and performs continuous matching of after-hours pricing orders on a time priority basis.
3.8 From 9:15 to 15:05 each trading day, after-hours pricing orders are not included in real-time market data; from 15:05 to 15:30, after-hours pricing orders and transactions are included in real-time market data. Real-time market data includes: security code, security abbreviation, closing price, cumulative trading volume of after-hours pricing transactions for the day, cumulative trading value of after-hours pricing transactions for the day, and the number of real-time unexecuted buy or sell orders.
3.9 The trading volume and value of after-hours pricing transactions are included in the total trading volume and total trading value of the stock on that day after the after-hours pricing transactions conclude.
3.10 Shares sold through after-hours pricing transactions shall be deemed to have been sold through competitive bidding transactions.
3.11 After-hours pricing transactions are subject to the Shenzhen-Hong Kong Stock Connect quota control. If the daily quota is exhausted during the after-hours pricing transactions on the Hong Kong Stock Exchange, the Stock Exchange Securities Trading Service Company will cease accepting subsequent buy orders for the day, but will still accept sell orders. Once buy orders are suspended, they will not be resumed on the same day, unless otherwise stipulated by the Exchange.
Chapter Four: Public Information on Transactions
4.1 In the event of any of the following circumstances in the competitive bidding trading of ChiNext stocks with price fluctuation limits, the Exchange shall publish the names of the five member securities business departments or trading units with the largest buy and sell amounts for the relevant stocks on that day, along with their respective buy and sell amounts: (i) the top five stocks whose closing price fluctuation reaches ±15% on that day; (ii) the top five stocks whose price volatility reaches 30% on that day, calculated as follows: Price volatility = (Highest price of the day - Lowest price of the day) / Lowest price of the day × 100%; (iii) the top five stocks whose turnover rate reaches 30% on that day, calculated as follows: Turnover rate = Number of shares traded / Total number of shares without trading restrictions × 100%. If the closing price fluctuation, price volatility, or turnover rate are the same, the selection shall be based on the transaction amount and transaction volume in that order.
4.2 The following situations constitute abnormal fluctuations in the competitive bidding trading of ChiNext stocks, and the Exchange will publish the names of the five member securities business departments or trading units with the largest cumulative purchase and sale amounts during the period of abnormal fluctuations, as well as their respective cumulative purchase and sale amounts: (i) The cumulative deviation of the daily closing price change reaches ±30% within three consecutive trading days. The formula for calculating the deviation of the closing price change is: Deviation of closing price change = Change of single stock - Change of ChiNext Composite Index; (ii) Other situations that the China Securities Regulatory Commission or the Exchange deems to be abnormal fluctuations.
4.3 Any of the following situations in the competitive bidding trading of ChiNext stocks constitutes a serious abnormal fluctuation, and the Exchange will publish investor classification and trading statistics during the period of serious abnormal fluctuation: (i) Three instances of the same-direction abnormal fluctuation as stipulated in Article 4.2 within 10 consecutive trading days; (ii) The cumulative deviation of the daily closing price change reaches 100% (-50%) within 10 consecutive trading days; (iii) The cumulative deviation of the daily closing price change reaches +200% (-70%) within 30 consecutive trading days; (iv) Other situations deemed serious abnormal fluctuations by the China Securities Regulatory Commission or the Exchange. If multiple situations of serious abnormal fluctuations occur in ChiNext stock trading, the Exchange will publish them together.
4.4 In the event of severe abnormal fluctuations in the trading of ChiNext stocks, the Exchange may, depending on market conditions, strengthen the monitoring of abnormal transactions and require members to take effective measures to alert clients to the risks.
4.5 The abnormal fluctuation and severe abnormal fluctuation indicators will be recalculated from the date of this announcement. This exchange may adjust the criteria for identifying abnormal fluctuation and severe abnormal fluctuation based on market conditions. Stocks without price fluctuation limits are not included in the calculation of abnormal fluctuation and severe abnormal fluctuation indicators.
Chapter Five Other Transaction Matters
5.1 The Exchange may, in an appropriate manner, mark the shares or depositary receipts of issuers that are not profitable at the time of listing, have differential voting rights arrangements, contractual control structures, or similar special arrangements.
5.2 Stocks issued and listed in accordance with the "Trial Measures for the Registration Management of Initial Public Offerings of Stocks on the Growth Enterprise Market" may be used as margin trading and securities lending targets from the first day of listing. If a stock is subject to risk warning, the Exchange will remove it from the scope of target securities from the date the risk warning is implemented.
5.3 The minimum price fluctuation unit for ChiNext stocks is subject to the relevant provisions of the Trading Rules. The Exchange may implement different minimum price fluctuation units based on the stock price level; specific details will be stipulated separately by the Exchange.
5.4 Matters related to the trading of ChiNext depositary receipts, margin trading, and securities lending shall be handled in accordance with the provisions on stock trading in these regulations, the Trading Rules, and other relevant regulations of the Exchange.
